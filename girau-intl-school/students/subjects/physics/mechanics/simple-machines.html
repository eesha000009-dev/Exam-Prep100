1 What are Simple Machines?

A simple machine is a device that makes work easier by changing the magnitude or direction of a force.

Simple machines do NOT reduce the amount of work - they make work easier by allowing us to use less force over a greater distance or change the direction of force.

What Machines Do

  • • Multiply force (force multiplier)
  • • Multiply speed/distance
  • • Change direction of force
  • • Transfer force from one place to another

The Six Types

  1. Levers
  2. Pulleys
  3. Inclined planes
  4. Wedges
  5. Screws
  6. Wheel and axle

2 Important Terms & Formulas

Mechanical Advantage (MA)

MA = Load ÷ Effort

The ratio of load to effort. MA > 1 means the machine multiplies force.

Velocity Ratio (VR)

VR = Distance moved by effort ÷ Distance moved by load

Also called "Ideal Mechanical Advantage" - the theoretical advantage based on geometry.

Efficiency (η)

Efficiency = (MA ÷ VR) × 100%

How well the machine works. Always less than 100% due to friction.

Key Relationship

Work input = Work output (ignoring friction)
Effort × Distanceeffort = Load × Distanceload

3 Types of Simple Machines

1. Levers

A rigid bar that rotates around a fixed point called the fulcrum.

Class 1: Fulcrum in middle (seesaw, scissors)
Class 2: Load in middle (wheelbarrow, nutcracker)
Class 3: Effort in middle (tweezers, fishing rod)

VR = Distance from effort to fulcrum ÷ Distance from load to fulcrum

2. Pulleys

A wheel with a groove that holds a rope or cable. Used to lift loads.

  • • Single fixed pulley: VR = 1 (changes direction only)
  • • Single movable pulley: VR = 2
  • • Block and tackle: VR = number of pulleys

3. Inclined Plane

A flat surface tilted at an angle to the horizontal. Makes lifting easier.

VR = Length of slope ÷ Height of slope

Examples: Ramp, slide, mountain road

4. Wheel and Axle

A large wheel attached to a smaller axle that turn together.

VR = Radius of wheel ÷ Radius of axle

Examples: Steering wheel, doorknob, bicycle wheel

5. Wedge & Screw

Wedge: Two inclined planes joined together (axe, knife, chisel)

Screw: Inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder (jar lid, bolt, drill)

Practice Questions

1. A machine has a load of 200 N lifted by an effort of 50 N. Calculate the MA.

Show Answer
MA = Load ÷ Effort = 200 N ÷ 50 N = 4

2. An inclined plane is 6 m long and 2 m high. Calculate its VR.

Show Answer
VR = Length of slope ÷ Height = 6 m ÷ 2 m = 3

3. A pulley system has MA = 4 and VR = 5. Calculate its efficiency.

Show Answer
Efficiency = (MA ÷ VR) × 100% = (4 ÷ 5) × 100% = 80%

4. Give one reason why a machine's efficiency is always less than 100%.

Show Answer
Some energy is lost as heat due to:
  • Friction between moving parts
  • Work done against air resistance
  • Energy used to lift parts of the machine itself

Key Takeaways

  • • Simple machines make work easier but don't reduce total work
  • • MA = Load ÷ Effort; VR = Distanceeffort ÷ Distanceload
  • • Efficiency = (MA ÷ VR) × 100% (always less than 100% due to friction)
  • • Six types: Lever, Pulley, Inclined plane, Wedge, Screw, Wheel & axle
  • • Machines trade force for distance - use less force over greater distance
