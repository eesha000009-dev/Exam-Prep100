Rational Numbers

Understand rational numbers, their properties, and how to convert between fractions and decimals - essential concepts for WAEC mathematics.

Learning Objectives

  • • Define and identify rational numbers
  • • Convert between fractions and terminating/repeating decimals
  • • Simplify fractions to their lowest terms
  • • Compare and order rational numbers

1 Definition of Rational Numbers

A rational number is any number that can be expressed as a fraction p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.

Rational Number = p/q

where p, q are integers and q ≠ 0

Examples of Rational Numbers

  • • Integers: 5 = 5/1, -3 = -3/1
  • • Fractions: 3/4, -2/5, 17/8
  • • Terminating decimals: 0.75 = 3/4
  • • Repeating decimals: 0.333... = 1/3

NOT Rational Numbers (Irrational)

  • • π (pi): ≈ 3.14159... (non-repeating)
  • • √2: ≈ 1.41421... (non-repeating)
  • • √3: ≈ 1.73205... (non-repeating)
  • • e: ≈ 2.71828... (non-repeating)

Key Insight

If a decimal terminates (ends) OR repeats in a pattern, it IS a rational number and can be written as a fraction!

2 Converting Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

Terminating Decimals

These end after a finite number of decimal places.

1/2 = 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5

3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

5/8 = 5 ÷ 8 = 0.625

Repeating Decimals

These have a pattern that repeats forever.

1/3 = 0.333... = 0.3̄

2/3 = 0.666... = 0.6̄

1/7 = 0.142857142857... = 0.1̄4̅2̅8̅5̅7̅

Problem: Convert 5/12 to a decimal.

Method: Long division of 5 ÷ 12

5.000000 ÷ 12
= 0.416666...
= 0.416̄

Verification:

5/12 = (5 × 8.333...) / 12 = 0.41666...
Or: 12 × 0.41666... = 4.999... ≈ 5

Answer: 5/12 = 0.416̄ (read as "0.416 recurring")

3 Converting Decimals to Fractions

Terminating Decimals

Write the decimal as a fraction over a power of 10, then simplify.

0.6 = 6/10 = 3/5

0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4

0.125 = 125/1000 = 1/8

Problem: Express 0.375 as a fraction in its simplest form.

Step 1: Write as fraction over power of 10

0.375 has 3 decimal places, so: 375/1000

Step 2: Find the GCD (Greatest Common Divisor)

GCD of 375 and 1000 = 125
(Both divisible by 5: 375 = 5³ × 3, 1000 = 5³ × 8)

Step 3: Divide numerator and denominator by GCD

375 ÷ 125 = 3
1000 ÷ 125 = 8
Therefore: 3/8

Answer: 0.375 = 3/8

Converting Repeating Decimals to Fractions

Use algebra to convert repeating decimals:

Example: Convert 0.7̄ to a fraction

Let x = 0.7777...

Then 10x = 7.7777...

10x - x = 7.7777... - 0.7777...

9x = 7, therefore x = 7/9

Answer: 0.7̄ = 7/9

4 Simplifying Fractions

A fraction is in its simplest form (lowest terms) when the numerator and denominator have no common factor other than 1.

Method: Divide by the GCD

Find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of numerator and denominator, then divide both by it.

Problem: Simplify 45/60 to its lowest terms.

Method 1: Step by step division

45/60 = (÷5) → 9/12
9/12 = (÷3) → 3/4
3/4 is in lowest terms (GCD of 3 and 4 is 1)

Method 2: Find GCD directly

45 = 3² × 5
60 = 2² × 3 × 5
GCD = 3 × 5 = 15
45/60 = (45÷15)/(60÷15) = 3/4

Answer: 45/60 = 3/4

5 Comparing and Ordering Rational Numbers

Method 1: Common Denominator

Convert all fractions to have the same denominator.

Compare 3/4 and 5/6:

LCM = 12: 9/12 vs 10/12

5/6 > 3/4

Method 2: Convert to Decimals

Convert all to decimals and compare.

Compare 2/5, 0.45, and 4/9:

2/5 = 0.4

4/9 = 0.444...

Order: 0.4 < 0.444... < 0.45
