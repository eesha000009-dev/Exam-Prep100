Financial Arithmetic

Master simple and compound interest, discounts, depreciation, and basic annuities - essential skills for WAEC success and real-world financial literacy.

Learning Objectives

  • • Calculate simple interest using the formula I = PRT
  • • Calculate compound interest and final amounts
  • • Apply discount and depreciation calculations
  • • Solve problems involving hire purchase and amortization

1 Simple Interest

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal amount. It's straightforward and commonly used for short-term loans.

Simple Interest Formula

I = P × R × T

Where: I = Interest, P = Principal, R = Rate (as decimal), T = Time (years)

Problem: Calculate the simple interest on ₦8,000 invested at 5% per annum for 3 years.

Step 1: Identify the values

  • Principal (P) = ₦8,000
  • Rate (R) = 5% = 0.05 (convert to decimal)
  • Time (T) = 3 years

Step 2: Apply the formula

I = P × R × T
I = 8,000 × 0.05 × 3
I = ₦1,200

Answer: The simple interest is ₦1,200

Problem: What sum of money invested at 4% per annum will yield ₦600 interest in 2½ years?

Step 1: Identify the values

  • Interest (I) = ₦600
  • Rate (R) = 4% = 0.04
  • Time (T) = 2.5 years

Step 2: Rearrange the formula to find P

P = I ÷ (R × T)
P = 600 ÷ (0.04 × 2.5)
P = 600 ÷ 0.1
P = ₦6,000

Answer: The principal is ₦6,000

2 Compound Interest

Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any accumulated interest. This is how most bank accounts and investments work.

Compound Interest Formula

A = P(1 + R)ⁿ

Where: A = Final Amount, P = Principal, R = Rate (as decimal), n = Number of periods

Key Difference

Simple Interest = Interest on principal only | Compound Interest = Interest on principal + accumulated interest. This is why compound interest grows faster over time!

Problem: Find the compound interest on ₦5,000 invested at 6% per annum for 3 years.

Step 1: Identify the values

  • Principal (P) = ₦5,000
  • Rate (R) = 6% = 0.06
  • Number of years (n) = 3

Step 2: Apply the formula

A = P(1 + R)ⁿ
A = 5,000 × (1 + 0.06)³
A = 5,000 × (1.06)³
A = 5,000 × 1.191016
A = ₦5,955.08

Step 3: Calculate the interest

Interest = A - P = ₦5,955.08 - ₦5,000 = ₦955.08

Answer: The compound interest is ₦955.08 (compare with simple interest: ₦900)

3 Discount and Depreciation

Discount

A reduction in the original price of an item.

Discount = Original Price × Discount Rate

Sale Price = Original Price - Discount

Depreciation

A decrease in the value of an asset over time.

Depreciated Value = Original Value × (1 - rate)ⁿ

Problem: A car bought for ₦2,000,000 depreciates at 15% per annum. Find its value after 4 years.

Step 1: Identify the values

  • Original Value = ₦2,000,000
  • Depreciation Rate = 15% = 0.15
  • Time (n) = 4 years

Step 2: Apply the depreciation formula

Value = 2,000,000 × (1 - 0.15)⁴
Value = 2,000,000 × (0.85)⁴
Value = 2,000,000 × 0.5220
Value = ₦1,044,000

Answer: The car's value after 4 years is ₦1,044,000

4 Hire Purchase

Hire purchase allows you to buy items by paying a deposit followed by installments. The total paid is usually more than the cash price.

Total Hire Purchase Price = Deposit + (Monthly Installments × Number of Months)

Problem: A television has a cash price of ₦120,000. It can also be bought on hire purchase by paying 20% deposit and 12 monthly installments of ₦10,000. Calculate the extra amount paid on hire purchase.

Step 1: Calculate the deposit

Deposit = 20% × 120,000 = ₦24,000

Step 2: Calculate total installments

Total Installments = 12 × 10,000 = ₦120,000

Step 3: Calculate total hire purchase price

HP Price = 24,000 + 120,000 = ₦144,000

Step 4: Calculate extra amount

Extra = 144,000 - 120,000 = ₦24,000

Answer: The extra amount paid is ₦24,000 (which is 20% of the cash price)

Real-World Applications

Banking

Banks use compound interest for savings accounts and loans. Understanding this helps you compare different financial products.

Shopping

Understanding discounts helps you calculate sale prices and compare "buy now, pay later" options.

Practice Problems

2. Calculate the amount after 3 years when ₦10,000 is invested at 5% compound interest annually.

3. A laptop costs ₦180,000 cash. On hire purchase, a 25% deposit is required plus 6 monthly payments of ₦26,000. How much extra is paid?
