Elements of Sentence Structure

Basic Elements

  • Subject (who/what performs the action)
  • Predicate (what is said about the subject)
  • Objects (direct and indirect)
  • Complements (subject and object complements)
  • Modifiers (words/phrases that add detail)

Worked Example

The energetic puppy playfully chased the red ball across the garden.

  • Subject: "The energetic puppy" (noun phrase)
  • Predicate: "playfully chased the red ball across the garden"
  • Direct Object: "the red ball"
  • Modifiers:
    • "energetic" (adjective modifying puppy)
    • "playfully" (adverb modifying chased)
    • "red" (adjective modifying ball)
    • "across the garden" (prepositional phrase)

Practice Questions

1. Identify all sentence elements in:

"My younger sister carefully placed her new book on the shelf."

2. Convert this sentence to include an indirect object:

"The teacher handed the assignment."

3. Add appropriate modifiers to enrich this basic sentence:

"The cat caught a mouse."

Brief Solutions

1. Subject: "My younger sister" Predicate: "carefully placed her new book on the shelf" Direct Object: "her new book" Modifiers: "younger", "carefully", "new", "on the shelf"

2. "The teacher handed the students the assignment." (added indirect object "the students")

3. "The stealthy cat quickly caught a tiny field mouse in the garden." (added adjectives, adverb, and prepositional phrase)
