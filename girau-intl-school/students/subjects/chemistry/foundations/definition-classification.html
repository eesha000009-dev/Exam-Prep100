Definition & Classification of Matter

Understanding what chemistry is and how matter is classified into elements, compounds, and mixtures.

Learning Objectives

  • • Define chemistry and matter
  • • Distinguish between elements, compounds, and mixtures
  • • Classify substances as elements, compounds, or mixtures
  • • Differentiate between homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures
  • • Understand the difference between metals and non-metals

1 What is Chemistry?

Chemistry

is the scientific study of the composition, structure, properties, and changes of matter.

Matter

Anything that has mass and occupies space. Examples: air, water, rocks, plants, metals.

Energy

The capacity to do work. Chemistry also studies energy changes during reactions (heat, light, etc.).

3 Elements

Definition

An element is a pure substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means. It consists of only one type of atom.

Metals

  • • Shiny (lustrous)
  • • Good conductors of heat & electricity
  • • Malleable (can be hammered)
  • • Ductile (can be drawn into wires)
  • • High melting points

Examples: Iron, Copper, Gold, Sodium

Non-Metals

  • • Dull (non-lustrous)
  • • Poor conductors
  • • Brittle (break easily)
  • • Low melting points
  • • Can be solids, liquids, or gases

Examples: Carbon, Oxygen, Chlorine, Sulfur

Metalloids

Elements with properties between metals and non-metals. Examples: Silicon, Germanium, Boron. Used in electronics (semiconductors).

4 Compounds

Definition

A compound is a pure substance formed when two or more elements combine chemically in fixed proportions.

Key Characteristics of Compounds:

  • Elements combine in fixed ratios by mass
  • Properties are different from constituent elements
  • Can only be separated by chemical means
  • Components are chemically bonded

Example: Water (H₂O)

Hydrogen (flammable gas) + Oxygen (supports burning) → Water (extinguishes fire)

The properties of water are completely different from hydrogen and oxygen!

Classify the following as elements or compounds: H₂O, O₂, NaCl, Fe, CO₂, N₂

Elements

O₂, Fe, N₂ - contain only one type of atom

Compounds

H₂O, NaCl, CO₂ - contain two or more different atoms chemically combined

5 Mixtures

Definition

A mixture consists of two or more substances physically combined. The components can be present in any proportion and can be separated by physical means.

Homogeneous Mixtures

Uniform throughout - cannot see individual components

Examples: Salt solution, air, alloys (bronze, steel), soft drinks

Also called "solutions"

Heterogeneous Mixtures

Non-uniform - can see different components

Examples: Sand and water, oil and water, salad, rocks

Components are visibly distinct

Key Differences from Compounds:

  • • Components can be in any proportion
  • • Can be separated by physical means (filtration, distillation, etc.)
  • • Properties are a combination of the components
  • • No chemical bonds between components

Practice Problems

1. Classify each as element, compound, or mixture: (a) Air, (b) Gold, (c) Carbon dioxide, (d) Salt solution

Show Solution

(a) Air - Mixture (homogeneous: N₂, O₂, CO₂, etc.)

(b) Gold - Element (pure substance, one type of atom)

(c) Carbon dioxide - Compound (C and O chemically combined)

(d) Salt solution - Mixture (homogeneous: salt dissolved in water)

2. Why is air classified as a mixture and not a compound?

Show Solution

Air is a mixture because:

  • Components can vary in proportion
  • Components can be separated physically (e.g., fractional distillation)
  • No chemical bonds between N₂, O₂, etc.
  • Components retain their individual properties

Key Takeaways for WAEC/JAMB/NECO

  • • Element: One type of atom, cannot be broken down further
  • • Compound: Fixed ratio, chemical bonds, separated chemically only
  • • Mixture: Variable ratio, no chemical bonds, separated physically
  • • Homogeneous: Uniform throughout (solutions, alloys)
  • • Heterogeneous: Non-uniform, visible components
  • • Know the differences between metals, non-metals, and metalloids
