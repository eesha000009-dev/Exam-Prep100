What's a derivative?

The derivative f'(x) gives the instantaneous rate of change or the gradient of the tangent at x. Power rule: d/dx[x^n] = n x^{n-1}.

Visual — tangent & slope

The tangent line touches the curve at a single point; its slope equals the derivative.

Worked examples

  1. If f(x)=x^3, f'(x)=3x^2. At x=2, f'(2)=12.
  2. Power rule: d/dx[5x^4]=20x^3.
  3. Product rule example: y = x^2(x+1). y' = x^2·1 + 2x·(x+1) = 3x^2 + 2x.
  4. Higher-order derivative: for f(x)=x^4, f'(x)=4x^3, f''(x)=12x^2.
