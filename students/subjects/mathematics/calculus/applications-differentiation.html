Applications of Differentiation

Use derivatives for finding maxima/minima, slopes of tangents, and rates of change problems.

Worked example

If f(x)= -x^2 + 4x, f'(x)= -2x + 4. Set to zero: -2x+4=0 → x=2 (max).

Practice

  1. Find stationary point of y = x^3 - 3x^2.
  2. Interpret derivative of position as velocity.

Brief solutions

  1. y' = 3x^2 - 6x = 3x(x-2) → stationary at x=0,2.
  2. Velocity = derivative of displacement with respect to time.
