Ordered vs Unordered

Permutations count arrangements where order matters; combinations count selections where order doesn't matter.

Illustration

Arrangement (permutation) of ABC choosing 2: AB, AC, BA, BC, CA, CB — 6 ways. Selection (combination) of 2 from ABC: AB, AC, BC — 3 ways.

Practice

1) Arrange 3 letters from ABCD (ordered)
2) Choose 2 from 5 (combinations)
