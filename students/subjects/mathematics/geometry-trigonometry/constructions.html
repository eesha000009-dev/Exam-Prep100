Ruler-and-Compass Constructions

Constructions are precise geometric procedures using only a straightedge and compass. We'll show clear steps for common constructions and give interactive hints.

Step-by-step Examples

Perpendicular bisector of segment AB:
  1. With centre A and B draw arcs with same radius > AB/2.
  2. Label intersection points P and Q; draw line PQ. PQ is the perpendicular bisector.
Angle bisector:
  1. From vertex draw arc cutting both sides at X and Y.
  2. With centres X and Y and same radius draw arcs that intersect at Z.
  3. Line from vertex to Z bisects the angle.
