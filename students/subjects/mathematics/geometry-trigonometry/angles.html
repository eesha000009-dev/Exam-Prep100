What is an angle?

An angle is the figure formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint (the vertex). We measure angles in degrees (°). Understanding angle relationships (complementary, supplementary, alternate, interior) helps solve many geometry problems.

Key Properties

  • Complementary: sum to 90°.
  • Supplementary: sum to 180°.
  • Angles on a straight line: sum to 180°.
  • Angles at a point: sum to 360°.
  • Alternate/Corresponding: equal when lines are parallel.

Worked Examples

Example 1
If an angle is supplementary to 110°, the angle = 180 - 110 = 70°.
Example 2
Two parallel lines cut by a transversal: alternate angle to 70° is 70°.
