Elevation & Depression

Using trigonometry to solve height and distance problems involving angles of elevation and depression.

Practice

  1. From a point 30 m from a tower, angle of elevation is 30°. Find tower height.
  2. From top of cliff 50 m high, angle of depression to a boat is 20°. Find horizontal distance to boat.

Brief solutions

  1. height = 30 tan30° ≈ 30 × 0.577 = 17.32 m.
  2. distance = 50 cot20° ≈ 50 × 2.747 = 137.35 m.
