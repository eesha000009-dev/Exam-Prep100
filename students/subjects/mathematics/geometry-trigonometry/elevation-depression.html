Idea

Angles of elevation and depression use right-triangle trig. Use sin, cos, tan with opposite/adjacent/hypotenuse to find heights or distances.

Worked Examples

Example 1
Point 30 m from tower, angle of elevation 30°. Height = 30·tan30° ≈ 17.32 m.
Example 2
Cliff 50 m, angle of depression 20° → horizontal distance = 50·cot20° ≈ 137.35 m.

Practice Problems

1) Point 40 m from pole, angle of elevation 35°. Find height (to 2 dp)
2) From cliff 60 m high, depression angle 25°. Find horizontal distance (to 2 dp)
