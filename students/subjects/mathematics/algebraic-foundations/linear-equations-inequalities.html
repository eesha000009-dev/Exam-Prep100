What are Linear Equations & Inequalities?

A linear equation is an equation where the variable (like x) has no exponent higher than 1. It graphs as a straight line. Example: 2x + 3 = 7.

An inequality compares two values using <, >, ≤, or ≥ instead of =. Example: x + 4 > 10.

Tip: Solving means finding the value of x that makes the equation or inequality true.

Key Methods

Isolate x by undoing addition/subtraction and then multiplication/division.

Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 7

  1. Subtract 3 from both sides: 2x = 4
  2. Divide both sides by 2: x = 2

Tip: Always do the same thing to both sides to keep the equation balanced.

A linear equation like y = mx + b graphs as a straight line. m is the slope, b is the y-intercept.

Example: y = 2x + 1 has slope 2 and crosses y-axis at 1.

Tip: Plot two points and draw a line through them.

Solve just like equations, but remember: if you multiply or divide by a negative, flip the inequality sign.

Example: Solve -2x < 6

  1. Divide both sides by -2: x > -3 (sign flips!)

Tip: Always flip the sign when multiplying/dividing by a negative.

Worked Examples (step-by-step)

Example 1: Solve 3x - 5 = 10
  1. Add 5 to both sides: 3x = 15
  2. Divide both sides by 3: x = 5
  3. Final answer: x = 5
Example 2: Graph y = -x + 2
  1. Slope is -1, y-intercept is 2
  2. Plot (0,2) and (1,1)
  3. Draw a straight line through the points
Example 3: Solve 2x + 4 ≥ 10
  1. Subtract 4: 2x ≥ 6
  2. Divide by 2: x ≥ 3
  3. Final answer: x ≥ 3
Example 4: Solve -x < 7
  1. Divide by -1 (flip sign): x > -7
  2. Final answer: x > -7
