What are Indices?

An index (plural: indices) shows how many times a base is multiplied by itself. We write a^n to mean a × a × ... × a (n times).

2^3
=>
8

Basic Laws of Indices

Product of powers

a^m × a^n = a^{m+n}

Quotient of powers

a^m ÷ a^n = a^{m-n} (a ≠ 0)

Power of a power

(a^m)^n = a^{m·n}

Power of a product

(ab)^n = a^n b^n

Negative and Fractional Indices

Negative index: a^{-n} = 1 / a^n (a ≠ 0).

Fractional index: a^{1/n} = n√a. More generally, a^{m/n} = (n√a)^m.

Tip: Convert fractional indices to roots to evaluate, and use negative indices to rewrite reciprocals.

Worked Examples

2^3 × 2^4
2^{3+4} = 2^7 = 128
(3^2)^3
3^{2×3} = 3^6 = 729
5^{-2}
1 / 5^2 = 1/25
16^{3/4}
(4√16)^3 = 2^3 = 8
