Ratios, Proportions & Rates

Understanding proportional relationships, unit rates, and scaling problems in mathematics.

Learning Objectives

  • • Understand the concept of ratio and how to simplify it
  • • Solve problems involving direct and inverse proportion
  • • Calculate and apply unit rates to real-world problems
  • • Use proportional reasoning to solve scaling problems

1 Understanding Ratios

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind. It shows how many times one quantity is contained in another.

Writing Ratios:

  • Using colon: 3:4 (read as "3 to 4")
  • Using fraction: 3/4
  • Using words: "3 to 4" or "3 is to 4"

Simplify the ratio 24:36

Step 1: Find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of 24 and 36

GCD(24, 36) = 12

Step 2: Divide both numbers by the GCD

24 ÷ 12 : 36 ÷ 12 = 2 : 3

Answer: 2:3

2 Proportions

A proportion is a statement that two ratios are equal. When two quantities are in proportion, changing one affects the other in a predictable way.

Direct Proportion

As one quantity increases, the other increases at the same rate.

Example: More items = more cost

Inverse Proportion

As one quantity increases, the other decreases.

Example: More workers = less time to complete a task

If 5 apples cost ₦300, how much will 8 apples cost?

Step 1: Find the cost per apple (unit rate)

₦300 ÷ 5 = ₦60 per apple

Step 2: Multiply by the required quantity

8 × ₦60 = ₦480

Alternative Method (Proportion):

5 : 8 = 300 : x

x = (8 × 300) ÷ 5 = ₦480

Answer: ₦480

If 6 workers can complete a job in 10 days, how long will it take 15 workers?

Step 1: Calculate total work (worker-days)

6 workers × 10 days = 60 worker-days

Step 2: Divide by new number of workers

60 ÷ 15 = 4 days

Answer: 4 days

More workers = less time (inverse relationship)

3 Rates and Unit Rates

A rate compares two quantities with different units. A unit rate is a rate per one unit.

Common Examples of Rates:

  • • Speed: km/h, m/s (distance per time)
  • • Unit price: ₦/kg, $/item (cost per quantity)
  • • Productivity: items/hour (output per time)
  • • Heart rate: beats/minute

A car travels 180 km in 3 hours. How far will it travel in 5 hours at the same speed?

Step 1: Find the rate (speed)

Speed = 180 km ÷ 3 h = 60 km/h

Step 2: Apply the rate to find distance

Distance = 60 km/h × 5 h = 300 km

Answer: 300 km

Practice Problems

1. Simplify the ratio 45:75

Show Solution

GCD(45, 75) = 15

45 ÷ 15 : 75 ÷ 15 = 3:5

2. If 3 pencils cost ₦120, find the cost of 7 pencils.

Show Solution

Unit cost = ₦120 ÷ 3 = ₦40 per pencil

Cost of 7 = 7 × ₦40 = ₦280

3. A car uses 15 litres of fuel to travel 225 km. How far can it travel on 20 litres?

Show Solution

Rate = 225 km ÷ 15 L = 15 km/L

Distance = 15 km/L × 20 L = 300 km

4. 4 men can paint a house in 6 days. How many days will 8 men take?

Show Solution

Total work = 4 × 6 = 24 man-days

Days with 8 men = 24 ÷ 8 = 3 days

5. Share ₦500 between A and B in the ratio 3:2

Show Solution

Total parts = 3 + 2 = 5

Value of one part = ₦500 ÷ 5 = ₦100

A gets: 3 × ₦100 = ₦300

B gets: 2 × ₦100 = ₦200

Key Takeaways

  • • A ratio compares quantities of the same kind; always simplify to lowest terms
  • • In direct proportion, both quantities increase together
  • • In inverse proportion, one quantity increases as the other decreases
  • • A unit rate is always expressed per ONE unit (e.g., km per 1 hour)
  • • Cross-multiplication is useful for solving proportion problems
