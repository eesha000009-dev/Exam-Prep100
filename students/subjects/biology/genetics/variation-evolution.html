Key Concepts

1. Variation in Populations

  • • Sources of variation:
    • - Genetic factors
    • - Environmental factors
    • - Gene combinations
  • • Types of variation:
    • - Continuous variation
    • - Discontinuous variation
  • • Population characteristics:
    • - Gene pools
    • - Allele frequencies

2. Natural Selection

  • • Darwin's observations:
    • - Overproduction
    • - Competition
    • - Survival advantages
  • • Selection process:
    • - Environmental pressures
    • - Differential survival
    • - Reproductive success
  • • Types of selection:
    • - Directional selection
    • - Stabilizing selection

3. Adaptation

  • • Physical adaptations:
    • - Body structures
    • - Coloration
    • - Size and shape
  • • Behavioral adaptations:
    • - Feeding strategies
    • - Mating behaviors
    • - Migration patterns
  • • Environmental fit:
    • - Habitat requirements
    • - Resource use

4. Evidence for Evolution

  • • Observable evidence:
    • - Fossil record
    • - Similar structures
    • - Shared features
  • • Modern examples:
    • - Antibiotic resistance
    • - Pesticide resistance
    • - Industrial melanism
