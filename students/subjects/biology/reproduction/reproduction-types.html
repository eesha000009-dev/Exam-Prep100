Key Concepts

1. Asexual Reproduction

  • • Types:
    • - Binary fission
    • - Budding
    • - Fragmentation
    • - Vegetative propagation
  • • Advantages:
    • - Rapid population growth
    • - Resource efficiency
    • - Single parent sufficient
  • • Examples in nature

2. Sexual Reproduction

  • • Key features:
    • - Gamete formation
    • - Fertilization
    • - Genetic recombination
  • • Advantages:
    • - Genetic variation
    • - Adaptation potential
    • - Evolution support
  • • Types of fertilization

3. Life Cycles

  • • Simple life cycles:
    • - Direct development
    • - Continuous growth
  • • Complex life cycles:
    • - Metamorphosis
    • - Alternation of generations
  • • Environmental influences

4. Reproductive Strategies

  • • R-selected species:
    • - Many offspring
    • - Limited parental care
  • • K-selected species:
    • - Few offspring
    • - Extended parental care
  • • Environmental adaptations

Self-Assessment

1. Compare the advantages and disadvantages of sexual and asexual reproduction.

Consider genetic diversity, resource requirements, and survival strategies.

2. Explain how different life cycles are adapted to different environments.

Use specific examples from various ecosystems.

3. Analyze the relationship between reproductive strategy and survival.

Compare r-selected and K-selected species.
