Key Concepts

1. Bacteria

  • • Structure:
    • - Cell wall composition
    • - Cellular organization
    • - Flagella and pili
  • • Classification:
    • - Gram-positive vs negative
    • - Shapes (cocci, bacilli, spirilla)
    • - Growth requirements
  • • Reproduction:
    • - Binary fission
    • - Growth phases
    • - Colony formation

2. Viruses

  • • Structure:
    • - Capsid
    • - Genetic material
    • - Envelope (when present)
  • • Classification:
    • - DNA vs RNA viruses
    • - Envelope presence
    • - Host range
  • • Replication:
    • - Host cell entry
    • - Viral assembly
    • - Release mechanisms

3. Fungi

  • • Structure:
    • - Cell wall composition
    • - Hyphae and mycelium
    • - Spore structures
  • • Types:
    • - Yeasts
    • - Molds
    • - Mushrooms
  • • Growth:
    • - Nutritional requirements
    • - Environmental conditions
    • - Reproduction methods

4. Protozoa

  • • Characteristics:
    • - Single-celled eukaryotes
    • - Movement methods
    • - Feeding mechanisms
  • • Classification:
    • - Amoebae
    • - Flagellates
    • - Ciliates
    • - Sporozoans
  • • Life Cycles:
    • - Reproduction methods
    • - Encystment
    • - Host relationships

Self-Assessment

1. Compare and contrast bacterial and viral structure.

Include key structural components and their functions.

2. Explain the different types of fungal organisms.

Discuss their structure and growth patterns.

3. Describe protozoan classification and characteristics.

Include examples of each major group.
