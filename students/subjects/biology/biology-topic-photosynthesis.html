1 What is Photosynthesis?

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. It is one of the most important biological processes on Earth!

Simple Definition

Photosynthesis is how plants make their own food using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water to produce glucose and oxygen.

Why is Photosynthesis Important?

  • • Produces oxygen for all living organisms to breathe
  • • Forms the base of most food chains on Earth
  • • Removes CO2 from the atmosphere (helps with climate regulation)
  • • Creates stored energy in the form of glucose (food)
  • • Source of fossil fuels (ancient photosynthesis)

2 The Equation for Photosynthesis

Word Equation:

Carbon Dioxide + Water → (Light + Chlorophyll) → Glucose + Oxygen

Chemical Equation:

6CO2 + 6H2O → (Light + Chlorophyll) → C6H12O6 + 6O2

Reactants (Inputs)

  • • Carbon Dioxide (CO2) - from the air
  • • Water (H2O) - absorbed by roots
  • • Light Energy - from the sun
  • • Chlorophyll - in chloroplasts

Products (Outputs)

  • • Glucose (C6H12O6) - food/energy
  • • Oxygen (O2) - released into air
  • • Water vapor (sometimes)

3 Leaf Structure for Photosynthesis

Leaves are perfectly adapted for photosynthesis. Here's how their structure supports this vital process:

Upper Epidermis

Transparent layer that allows light to pass through to the palisade layer below. Has a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Palisade Mesophyll

Tightly packed column-shaped cells near the upper surface. Contains many chloroplasts for maximum light absorption. Main site of photosynthesis!

Spongy Mesophyll

Loosely arranged cells with air spaces between them. Allows gas exchange (CO2 in, O2 out) through the stomata.

Stomata & Guard Cells

Tiny pores on the lower epidermis that open and close to control gas exchange and water loss. Guard cells control the opening.

Vascular Bundle (Veins)

Xylem transports water to leaf cells. Phloem transports glucose away from the leaf to other parts of the plant.

4 Factors Affecting the Rate of Photosynthesis

Several factors can limit the rate of photosynthesis. These are called limiting factors:

Light Intensity

More light = faster photosynthesis (up to a point). The rate levels off when another factor becomes limiting.

Carbon Dioxide Concentration

More CO2 = faster photosynthesis (up to a point). Greenhouses often add CO2 to increase crop yields.

Temperature

Rate increases with temperature until enzymes denature (usually above 40-45°C). Optimum is around 25-35°C for most plants.

Water Availability

Water shortage causes stomata to close, reducing CO2 uptake. Severe shortage stops photosynthesis entirely.

Liebig's Law of the Minimum

The rate of photosynthesis is limited by the factor that is in shortest supply. Even if light and temperature are perfect, low CO2 will limit the rate.

Practice Questions

1. Write the word equation for photosynthesis.

Show Answer
Carbon Dioxide + Water → (Light + Chlorophyll) → Glucose + Oxygen

2. Why are the palisade cells located near the upper surface of the leaf?

Show Answer
Palisade cells are near the upper surface to receive maximum light for photosynthesis. They are packed with chloroplasts and their position allows them to capture as much light energy as possible for the photosynthesis process.

3. Explain why a plant might stop growing in a closed container with no fresh air supply.

Show Answer
  1. The plant uses up the available CO2 in the container for photosynthesis
  2. Without fresh air, CO2 becomes a limiting factor
  3. Photosynthesis slows or stops, reducing glucose production
  4. Without glucose, the plant cannot grow or carry out cellular respiration

4. State the function of stomata in photosynthesis.

Show Answer
  • Allow CO2 to enter the leaf for photosynthesis
  • Allow O2 (produced during photosynthesis) to exit
  • Control water loss through transpiration
  • Open during the day for gas exchange, close at night to save water

Key Takeaways

  • • Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy (glucose)
  • • Equation: CO2 + H2O → (Light + Chlorophyll) → C6H12O6 + O2
  • • Leaf structure (palisade cells, stomata, veins) is adapted for efficient photosynthesis
  • • Limiting factors: light intensity, CO2 concentration, temperature, water
  • • Photosynthesis produces oxygen and food - essential for life on Earth
