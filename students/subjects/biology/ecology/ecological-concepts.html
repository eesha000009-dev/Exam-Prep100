Key Concepts

1. Levels of Organization

  • • Individual level:
    • - Single organisms
    • - Basic survival needs
  • • Population level:
    • - Groups of same species
    • - Population dynamics
  • • Community level:
    • - Different populations
    • - Species interactions
  • • Ecosystem level:
    • - Communities and environment
    • - Energy flow
  • • Biosphere:
    • - All Earth's ecosystems
    • - Global patterns

2. Environmental Factors

  • • Biotic factors:
    • - Living organisms
    • - Their interactions
    • - Population effects
  • • Abiotic factors:
    • - Temperature
    • - Water availability
    • - Light intensity
    • - Soil composition
    • - pH levels

3. Energy Flow

  • • Energy sources:
    • - Sunlight
    • - Chemical energy
  • • Energy transfer:
    • - Producers
    • - Consumers
    • - Decomposers
  • • Energy loss:
    • - Heat
    • - Movement
    • - Life processes

4. Ecological Relationships

  • • Competition:
    • - Resources
    • - Space
    • - Survival needs
  • • Symbiotic relationships:
    • - Mutualism
    • - Commensalism
    • - Parasitism
  • • Predator-prey relationships:
    • - Population cycles
    • - Adaptations

Self-Assessment

1. Describe the levels of ecological organization.

Use examples to illustrate each level.

2. Compare biotic and abiotic factors in an ecosystem.

Explain how they interact and affect organisms.

3. Analyze different types of ecological relationships.

Provide examples of each type of interaction.
