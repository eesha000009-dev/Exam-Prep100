Key Concepts

1. Types of Habitats

  • • Terrestrial Habitats:
    • - Forests (tropical, temperate, boreal)
    • - Grasslands and savannas
    • - Deserts
    • - Tundra
  • • Aquatic Habitats:
    • - Marine ecosystems
    • - Freshwater systems
    • - Wetlands
    • - Coral reefs
  • • Transitional Zones:
    • - Estuaries
    • - Mangroves
    • - Coastal regions

2. Habitat Characteristics

  • • Physical Factors:
    • - Temperature ranges
    • - Precipitation patterns
    • - Soil or water composition
    • - Light availability
  • • Biological Features:
    • - Species diversity
    • - Food availability
    • - Niche partitioning

3. Habitat Conservation

  • • Major Threats:
    • - Habitat fragmentation
    • - Climate change
    • - Pollution
    • - Human development
  • • Conservation Strategies:
    • - Protected areas
    • - Habitat corridors
    • - Species management
    • - Policy and legislation

4. Habitat Restoration

  • • Restoration Techniques:
    • - Native species reintroduction
    • - Invasive species control
    • - Ecosystem reconstruction
  • • Monitoring and Management:
    • - Success indicators
    • - Adaptive management
    • - Long-term monitoring
