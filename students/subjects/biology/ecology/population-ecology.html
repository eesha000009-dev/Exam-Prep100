Key Concepts

1. Population Growth

  • • Growth Patterns:
    • - Exponential growth
    • - Logistic growth
    • - Carrying capacity
  • • Growth Rate Factors:
    • - Birth rates
    • - Death rates
    • - Immigration
    • - Emigration
  • • Population Models:
    • - J-shaped curve
    • - S-shaped curve
    • - Population cycles

2. Limiting Factors

  • • Density-Dependent Factors:
    • - Competition
    • - Predation
    • - Disease
    • - Parasitism
  • • Density-Independent Factors:
    • - Natural disasters
    • - Climate changes
    • - Human activities

3. Population Interactions

  • • Competition:
    • - Intraspecific
    • - Interspecific
    • - Resource partitioning
  • • Predator-Prey Dynamics:
    • - Population cycles
    • - Adaptive strategies
    • - Coevolution
  • • Symbiotic Relationships:
    • - Mutualism
    • - Commensalism
    • - Parasitism

4. Population Regulation

  • • Natural Controls:
    • - Food availability
    • - Territory size
    • - Natural enemies
  • • Environmental Resistance:
    • - Resource limitation
    • - Environmental stress
    • - Population pressure

Self-Assessment

1. Compare exponential and logistic growth patterns.

Explain the role of carrying capacity in population growth.

2. Analyze density-dependent and density-independent factors.

Provide examples and explain their effects on populations.

3. Describe different types of population interactions.

Include competition, predation, and symbiotic relationships.
