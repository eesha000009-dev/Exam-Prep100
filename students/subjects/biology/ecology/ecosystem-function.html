Key Concepts

1. Nutrient Cycles

  • • Carbon Cycle:
    • - Photosynthesis
    • - Respiration
    • - Decomposition
    • - Carbon storage
  • • Nitrogen Cycle:
    • - Nitrogen fixation
    • - Nitrification
    • - Denitrification
  • • Water Cycle:
    • - Evaporation
    • - Precipitation
    • - Transpiration

2. Energy Flow

  • • Trophic Levels:
    • - Producers
    • - Primary consumers
    • - Secondary consumers
    • - Tertiary consumers
  • • Energy Transfer:
    • - 10% rule
    • - Energy pyramids
    • - Food chains
    • - Food webs

3. Ecosystem Productivity

  • • Primary Productivity:
    • - Gross primary productivity
    • - Net primary productivity
    • - Factors affecting productivity
  • • Secondary Productivity:
    • - Consumer biomass
    • - Growth efficiency
    • - Production rates

4. Ecosystem Stability

  • • Resilience:
    • - Recovery from disturbance
    • - Adaptation capacity
  • • Resistance:
    • - Ability to withstand change
    • - Buffering mechanisms
  • • Biodiversity:
    • - Species richness
    • - Functional diversity
    • - Ecosystem services

Self-Assessment

1. Explain how energy flows through an ecosystem.

Include the role of different trophic levels and energy transfer efficiency.

2. Describe the major nutrient cycles in an ecosystem.

Focus on carbon, nitrogen, and water cycles.

3. Analyze factors affecting ecosystem stability.

Discuss resilience, resistance, and the role of biodiversity.
