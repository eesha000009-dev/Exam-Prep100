Key Concepts

1. Neuron Structure

  • • Main components:
    • - Cell body (soma)
    • - Dendrites
    • - Axon
    • - Axon terminals
  • • Types of neurons:
    • - Sensory neurons
    • - Motor neurons
    • - Interneurons
  • • Supporting cells:
    • - Schwann cells
    • - Myelin sheath

2. Nerve Impulse Conduction

  • • Resting potential:
    • - Ion concentrations
    • - Membrane permeability
  • • Action potential:
    • - Depolarization
    • - Repolarization
    • - Refractory period
  • • Saltatory conduction in myelinated neurons

3. Synaptic Transmission

  • • Synapse structure:
    • - Presynaptic terminal
    • - Synaptic cleft
    • - Postsynaptic membrane
  • • Neurotransmitters:
    • - Types and functions
    • - Release and reuptake
    • - Receptor binding
  • • Types of synapses:
    • - Excitatory
    • - Inhibitory

4. Reflex Arcs

  • • Components:
    • - Receptor
    • - Sensory neuron
    • - Integration center
    • - Motor neuron
    • - Effector
  • • Types of reflexes:
    • - Simple reflexes
    • - Withdrawal reflexes
    • - Stretch reflexes
  • • Importance in survival

Self-Assessment

1. Explain how an action potential is generated and propagated.

Include ion movements and membrane changes.

2. Describe the process of synaptic transmission.

Include neurotransmitter release and receptor activation.

3. Analyze the components and function of a reflex arc.

Use a specific example like the knee-jerk reflex.
