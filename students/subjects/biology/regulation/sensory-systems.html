Sensory Systems

Understanding how organisms detect and process environmental stimuli

Learning Objectives

  • • Understand the structure and function of different sensory receptors
  • • Explain how the eye processes visual information
  • • Describe the mechanism of hearing and balance
  • • Analyze touch, taste, and smell reception

Key Concepts

1. The Eye and Vision

  • • Eye structure:
    • - Cornea and lens
    • - Retina and photoreceptors
    • - Rods and cones
  • • Image formation:
    • - Light refraction
    • - Accommodation
    • - Visual processing
  • • Common vision problems:
    • - Short-sightedness
    • - Long-sightedness
    • - Color blindness

2. The Ear and Hearing

  • • Ear structure:
    • - Outer ear
    • - Middle ear
    • - Inner ear
  • • Sound processing:
    • - Sound wave conduction
    • - Hair cell stimulation
    • - Neural signals
  • • Balance mechanism:
    • - Semicircular canals
    • - Vestibular system

3. Touch Reception

  • • Types of receptors:
    • - Mechanoreceptors
    • - Thermoreceptors
    • - Pain receptors
  • • Skin structure:
    • - Epidermis
    • - Dermis
    • - Receptor distribution
  • • Signal processing

4. Chemical Senses

  • • Taste (Gustation):
    • - Taste buds
    • - Basic tastes
    • - Taste processing
  • • Smell (Olfaction):
    • - Olfactory epithelium
    • - Odor detection
    • - Neural pathways
  • • Taste-smell interaction
