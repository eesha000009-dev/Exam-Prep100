Phrases & Clauses

Spot noun phrases, verb phrases, and distinguish independent from dependent clauses.

Worked example

Identify the clause type: "Although she was tired, she completed the homework."

Solution: "Although she was tired" — dependent (subordinate) clause; "she completed the homework" — independent clause.

Practice

  1. Find the noun phrase: "The tall man in the blue coat"
  2. Decide clause type: "Because it rained, the match was postponed."

Brief answers

  1. Noun phrase: "The tall man in the blue coat"
  2. Dependent clause: "Because it rained"; Independent: "the match was postponed."
