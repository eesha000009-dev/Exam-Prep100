Worked Example

1. Syllable Analysis of "determination":

  • de•ter•mi•na•tion (5 syllables)
  • Primary stress: -na- (fourth syllable)
  • Secondary stress: de- (first syllable)
  • Pattern: CV•CVC•CV•CV•CVC

2. Intonation Pattern:

"Are you coming to the party?" (rising)

"I'd love to, but I'm busy." (fall-rise on 'love', falling on 'busy')

Practice Questions

1. Mark syllable boundaries and stress in:

photographic, elementary, consideration

2. Draw intonation arrows for:

"Would you like coffee, tea, or juice?"

"I don't think so."

3. Identify the stress shift in:

PHOtograph → phoTOGraphy → photoGRAphic

Brief Solutions

1. pho•to•GRAPH•ic, e•le•MEN•ta•ry, con•si•de•RA•tion

2. List with rising tone on each item except last (falling)

3. Stress moves progressively rightward as suffixes are added
