Advanced Sentence Building

Techniques for Cohesion & Flow

Moving beyond basic sentences requires mastering techniques that create sophisticated, well-connected prose. This involves using a variety of structures and transitions to guide the reader smoothly through your ideas.

  • Parallelism: Using similar grammatical structures for related ideas.
  • Subordination: Emphasizing a main idea by placing less important information in dependent clauses.
  • Coordination: Joining ideas of equal importance with conjunctions.
  • Sentence Variety: Mixing simple, compound, and complex sentences to avoid monotony.
  • Transitional Phrases: Using words and phrases to signal relationships between sentences and paragraphs (e.g., "consequently," "in contrast," "furthermore").

Worked Example

Original choppy sentences:

The company launched a new product. It was innovative. The marketing campaign was aggressive. Sales were initially slow. They eventually increased.

Revised with advanced techniques:

Although the company launched an innovative new product and ran an aggressive marketing campaign, sales were initially slow before eventually increasing.

Analysis:

  • Subordination: The main clause ("sales were initially slow") is emphasized, while the product launch and marketing are placed in a subordinate "Although" clause.
  • Coordination: "launched an innovative new product" and "ran an aggressive marketing campaign" are joined with "and" to show they are related actions.
  • Sentence Variety: A single, complex sentence replaces five simple sentences, creating a more fluid and analytical tone.

Practice Questions

1. Combine these sentences into one using parallelism:

"To succeed, you need to study hard. It is also important to manage your time wisely. You should also seek help when needed."

2. Rewrite this passage to improve sentence variety and flow:

"The city council approved the new park. The park will have a playground. It will also have a community garden. Construction will begin next month. The park should be open by summer."

3. Use subordination to combine these ideas, emphasizing the second point:

"The experiment was carefully designed. It failed to produce the expected results."

Brief Solutions

1. "To succeed, you need to study hard, manage your time wisely, and seek help when needed."

2. "After approving the new park, which will feature a playground and a community garden, the city council announced that construction will begin next month. If all goes as planned, the park should be open by summer."

3. "Although the experiment was carefully designed, it failed to produce the expected results."
