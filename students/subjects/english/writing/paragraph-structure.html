Paragraph Structure

Key Elements

  • Topic sentence (main idea)
  • Supporting sentences (examples, details)
  • Concluding sentence (summary/transition)
  • Unity (single focus)
  • Coherence (logical flow)
  • Cohesion devices
    • Pronouns and references
    • Transitional expressions
    • Repetition of key terms
    • Parallel structures

Worked Example

Remote work has fundamentally changed modern office culture. Companies now operate with distributed teams across multiple time zones, using digital tools for collaboration and communication. This shift has led to increased flexibility for employees and reduced overhead costs for businesses. Additionally, studies show that remote workers often report higher job satisfaction and improved work-life balance. However, this new work model also presents challenges in maintaining team cohesion and corporate culture.

Analysis:

  • Topic sentence: States main idea about remote work's impact
  • Supporting details: Examples of changes, benefits
  • Concluding sentence: Balanced view with challenges
  • Cohesion devices:
    • "This shift" refers back to previous sentence
    • "Additionally" links related ideas
    • "However" signals contrast

Practice Questions

1. Analyze the structure of this paragraph:

Air pollution poses serious health risks in urban areas. Vehicle emissions and industrial waste release harmful particles into the atmosphere. These pollutants can cause respiratory problems and cardiovascular diseases. Furthermore, children and elderly people are particularly vulnerable to these health effects. Thus, reducing air pollution must be a priority for city planners and policymakers.

2. Improve the cohesion in this paragraph by adding appropriate transitions and references:

Plastic waste is a global problem. Plastic takes hundreds of years to decompose. Marine life is threatened by plastic. Microplastics enter the food chain. Humans consume plastic particles through seafood.

Brief Solutions

1. Topic: health risks of air pollution Support: sources, effects, vulnerable groups Conclusion: call for action Cohesion: Furthermore, Thus

2. Improved version: "Plastic waste is a global environmental crisis. These non-biodegradable materials take hundreds of years to decompose. As a result, marine life is increasingly threatened by plastic debris. Moreover, when plastic breaks down, microplastics enter the food chain. Consequently, humans now regularly consume plastic particles through contaminated seafood."
