Density & Relative Density

Definition, units, methods of measurement and applications.

Density

Density ρ = mass/volume. SI unit: kg m⁻³. Commonly used units: g cm⁻³.

  • Mass measured with balance
  • Volume measured directly (regular shapes) or by displacement (irregular solids)
  • Density varies with temperature and pressure for gases and liquids

Relative Density (Specific Gravity)

Relative density (specific gravity) = density of substance / density of water (at 4°C). Dimensionless quantity.

Example: If density of oil = 800 kg/m³, relative density = 800/1000 = 0.8

Measurement Methods

  1. Regular solids: measure dimensions and compute volume
  2. Irregular solids: use displacement method (measuring cylinder)
  3. Liquids: use hydrometer or pycnometer
  4. Gases: use ideal gas relationships and measured mass in known volume

Applications & Problems

Problem: Density from displacement

Weigh a solid in air and then when fully submerged in water; use weight loss to determine volume and hence density.

Application: Material Selection

Density helps choose materials for buoyancy, construction and transport.
