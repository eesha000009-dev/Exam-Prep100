Deformation of Solids

Elastic & plastic deformation, Hooke's law, stress-strain curves and strain energy.

Elastic vs Plastic Deformation

Elastic deformation is reversible on removal of load; plastic deformation is permanent.

  • Elastic limit: maximum stress for reversible deformation
  • Yield point: stress where plastic deformation begins
  • Fracture point: material breaks

Hooke's Law

Within elastic limit, extension is proportional to load: F = kx where k is spring constant.

Spring constant k = F/Δx. Energy stored in spring = ½ k x².

Stress-Strain Curve

Graphical representation of material response to increasing stress; important regions: elastic, yield, strain hardening, necking and fracture.

  • Proportional limit (linear region)
  • Elastic limit and yield point
  • Ultimate tensile strength and fracture

Strain Energy

Energy stored per unit volume when material is elastically deformed: U = ½ σε = ½ Y ε² (for linear behaviour), where σ is stress and ε is strain.
