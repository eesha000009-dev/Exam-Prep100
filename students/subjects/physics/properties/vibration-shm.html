Vibration in Springs & Pipes — Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Oscillatory motion of springs and air columns (pipes). Equations for SHM, period, frequency and energy in oscillation.

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

SHM is oscillation where acceleration is proportional and opposite to displacement: a = -ω²x. Solution: x(t)=A cos(ωt+φ).

  • Angular frequency ω = 2πf = √(k/m) for mass-spring systems
  • Period T = 2π/ω = 2π√(m/k)
  • Frequency f = 1/T

Mass-Spring System

Restoring force F = -kx; equation of motion: m d²x/dt² + kx = 0; energy oscillates between kinetic and potential forms.

Total energy E = ½kA² = ½mω²A² (constant in absence of damping).

Vibrations in Pipes (Air Columns)

Open and closed pipes support standing waves with specific harmonic series.

  • Open pipe: harmonics nλ/2 = L (n=1,2,3...)
  • Closed pipe: odd harmonics only (n=1,3,5...); nλ/4 = L
  • Frequency f = v/λ where v is speed of sound

Damping & Resonance (Overview)

Real systems have damping; resonance occurs when driving frequency matches natural frequency, causing large amplitude.
