Elastic Properties of Solids

Stress, strain, Young's modulus, bulk modulus and shear modulus with examples.

Stress & Strain

Stress is internal force per unit area; Strain is fractional change in dimensions.

  • Normal stress (tensile/compressive): σ = F/A
  • Shear stress: τ = F/A (parallel to area)
  • Linear strain: ε = ΔL / L

Young's Modulus

Young's modulus Y = stress / strain = (F/A) / (ΔL/L). Indicates stiffness of material.

Units: Pa (N m⁻²). Example values: Steel ≈ 200 GPa; Aluminium ≈ 70 GPa.

Bulk & Shear Modulus

Bulk modulus B measures change in volume under pressure; Shear modulus G measures deformation under shear stress.

  • Bulk modulus: B = -V (ΔP/ΔV)
  • Shear modulus: G = shear stress / shear strain
  • Relationships: E = 2G(1+ν), B = E/(3(1-2ν)) where ν is Poisson's ratio

Examples & Problems

Problem: Extension of a Wire

Given a wire of length L and cross-sectional area A under tension F, find extension ΔL using Young's modulus.

Application: Material Selection

Young's modulus helps decide materials for beams, cables and structural components where stiffness matters.
