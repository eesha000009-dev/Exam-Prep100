Field Concepts

Fields describe forces at a distance; electric and gravitational fields share similar mathematical structure: E = F/q, g = F/m.

Practice questions

  1. What is the electric field at a point 0.5 m from a 2 μC point charge? (Use k = 9×10^9 N·m^2·C^-2)
  2. How does field concept simplify analysis compared to treating forces from many sources directly?

Brief solutions

  1. E = kQ/r^2 = 9×10^9×2×10^-6 / 0.25 ≈ 7.2×10^4 N/C.
  2. Field allows superposition of fields (vectors) and avoids recomputing forces for each test charge, making many-body problems easier conceptually.
