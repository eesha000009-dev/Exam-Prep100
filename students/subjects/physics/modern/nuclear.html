Nuclear Physics Basics

Radioactivity, decay, half-life and binding energy at an introductory level.

Practice questions

  1. A sample contains 160 mg of a radioactive isotope with half-life 20 days. How much remains after 60 days?
  2. Define binding energy and its significance for nuclear stability.

Brief solutions

  1. After 60 days (3 half-lives) remaining = 160 / 2^3 = 20 mg.
  2. Binding energy is the energy required to separate a nucleus into its constituents; higher binding energy per nucleon usually means more stability.
