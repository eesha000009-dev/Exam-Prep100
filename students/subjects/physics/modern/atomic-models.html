Atomic Models

From Rutherford to Bohr and the quantum model—energy levels, spectral lines and atomic structure basics.

Worked example

An electron in the Bohr model for hydrogen moves between n=3 and n=2. The photon emitted has wavelength λ = ? (Use R = 1.097×10^7 m−1).

ΔE = Rhc(1/n_f^2 − 1/n_i^2) → λ = 1/ (R(1/4 − 1/9)) ≈ 656 nm

Practice questions

  1. Using the Bohr formula, calculate the wavelength for a transition n=4 to n=2 in hydrogen.
  2. Briefly explain why Rutherford's model could not explain discrete spectral lines.

Brief solutions

  1. Compute Δ(1/n^2)=1/4 − 1/16 = 3/16 → λ = 1/(R×3/16) ≈ 486 nm.
  2. Rutherford had no quantized energy levels; electrons in any orbit would radiate continuously and spectra would be continuous, not discrete.
