Energy Quantization & Duality

Photoelectric effect, quantized energy levels and the wave-particle duality concept (qualitative).

Worked example

If light of wavelength 400 nm hits a metal with work function 2.5 eV, what is the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons? (h = 4.14×10^-15 eV·s, c = 3×10^8 m/s)

E_photon = hc/λ ≈ 3.1 eV → K_max = 3.1 − 2.5 = 0.6 eV

Practice questions

  1. Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron with kinetic energy 150 eV.
  2. Explain qualitatively why increasing frequency (not intensity) can cause electron emission in the photoelectric effect.

Brief solutions

  1. Use λ = h/p and p = √(2mK). For 150 eV electron λ ≈ 0.1 nm (order of magnitude).
  2. Frequency raises photon energy; a single photon must have at least the work function energy to eject an electron, intensity only changes photon number.
