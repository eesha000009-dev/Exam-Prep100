1 What are Simple Machines?

A simple machine is a device that makes work easier by changing the magnitude or direction of a force. All complex machines are built from combinations of simple machines.

Key Concept

Simple machines do NOT reduce the total work done - they trade off force for distance. The same work is done, but with less effort over a greater distance.

What They Do

Multiply force, multiply speed, or change direction

Energy Conservation

Work input = Work output (ideal)

Six Types

Lever, pulley, inclined plane, wedge, screw, wheel & axle

2 Key Terms & Calculations

Mechanical Advantage (MA)

MA = Load / Effort

The ratio of output force to input force. Higher MA means less effort needed.

MA > 1: Force multiplier

MA < 1: Speed multiplier

Velocity Ratio (VR)

VR = dE / dL

The ratio of distance moved by effort to distance moved by load.

Also: VR = Distance effort / Distance load

Or: VR = Speed of effort / Speed of load

Efficiency (η)

η = (MA / VR) × 100%

How effectively a machine converts input work to output work.

η is always less than 100% due to friction

Important Relationships

  • • Ideal machine: MA = VR, Efficiency = 100%
  • • Real machines: MA < VR, Efficiency < 100% (due to friction)
  • • Work input = Effort × Distance effort moves
  • • Work output = Load × Distance load moves

3 Levers

A lever is a rigid bar that rotates around a fixed point called the fulcrum (pivot). It can multiply force or speed depending on the positions of load, effort, and fulcrum.

First Class

⚖️

Fulcrum between Load and Effort

Examples: Seesaw, scissors, crowbar, pliers

MA can be >1, =1, or <1

Second Class

🛒

Load between Fulcrum and Effort

Examples: Wheelbarrow, bottle opener, nutcracker

MA always > 1 (force multiplier)

Third Class

🏏

Effort between Fulcrum and Load

Examples: Fishing rod, tweezers, human arm, broom

MA < 1 (speed multiplier)

Lever Calculations

Velocity Ratio: VR = distance from effort to fulcrum / distance from load to fulcrum

Law of the Lever: Effort × Effort arm = Load × Load arm

4 Pulleys

A pulley is a wheel with a groove around its edge for a rope. Pulleys can change the direction of a force and multiply force when used in systems.

Single Fixed Pulley

🚡
  • • Changes direction of effort
  • • VR = 1, MA = 1
  • • No mechanical advantage
  • • Used for convenience (flagpole, well)

Single Movable Pulley

⭕
  • • Pulley moves with the load
  • • VR = 2, MA ≈ 2
  • • Doubles the lifting force
  • • Two rope segments support load

Block and Tackle (Pulley Systems)

Multiple pulleys combined for greater mechanical advantage:

2 pulleys

VR = 2

3 pulleys

VR = 3

4 pulleys

VR = 4

n pulleys

VR = n

Key: Count the number of rope segments supporting the load = VR

5 Inclined Plane & Wedge

Inclined Plane

A flat surface tilted at an angle to the horizontal. Makes lifting easier by increasing distance.

VR = Length / Height = 1 / sin(θ)

  • • Examples: Ramp, slide, mountain road
  • • Gentler slope = Higher VR = Less effort needed
  • • Trade-off: Move load greater distance

Wedge

A double inclined plane that moves. Converts input force to splitting force.

VR = Length / Width

  • • Examples: Axe, knife, chisel, nail
  • • Longer, thinner wedge = Higher VR
  • • Force applied to base, work done at tip

6 Screw & Wheel and Axle

Screw

An inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder. Converts rotational motion to linear motion.

VR = 2πr / p

r = radius of handle, p = pitch (distance between threads)

  • • Examples: Bolt, screw jack, drill, jar lid
  • • Smaller pitch = Higher VR = More force
  • • Very high mechanical advantage possible

Wheel and Axle

A larger wheel attached to a smaller axle. Both rotate together.

VR = R / r

R = radius of wheel, r = radius of axle

  • • Examples: Steering wheel, doorknob, wrench
  • • Larger wheel relative to axle = Higher VR
  • • Can multiply force or speed

Practice Questions

1. A lever has an effort arm of 80 cm and a load arm of 20 cm. Calculate the velocity ratio and the effort needed to lift a 200 N load.

Show Answer
Velocity Ratio:
VR = Effort arm / Load arm = 80 / 20 = 4

Effort (assuming 100% efficiency):
MA = VR = 4
Effort = Load / MA = 200 / 4 = 50 N

2. A pulley system has 4 pulleys. Calculate the effort required to lift a 600 N load if the system is 75% efficient.

Show Answer
Velocity Ratio: VR = 4 (4 pulleys)

Mechanical Advantage:
Efficiency = MA / VR
0.75 = MA / 4
MA = 3

Effort:
Effort = Load / MA = 600 / 3 = 200 N

3. An inclined plane is 5 m long and 1 m high. Calculate its velocity ratio. If a 100 N effort moves a 400 N load up the ramp, what is the efficiency?

Show Answer
Velocity Ratio:
VR = Length / Height = 5 / 1 = 5

Mechanical Advantage:
MA = Load / Effort = 400 / 100 = 4

Efficiency:
η = (MA / VR) × 100% = (4 / 5) × 100% = 80%

4. A wheel and axle has a wheel radius of 30 cm and an axle radius of 5 cm. Calculate the VR. If an effort of 50 N is applied, what load can be lifted (assuming 80% efficiency)?

Show Answer
Velocity Ratio:
VR = R / r = 30 / 5 = 6

Mechanical Advantage:
η = MA / VR
0.8 = MA / 6
MA = 4.8

Load:
Load = MA × Effort = 4.8 × 50 = 240 N

Key Takeaways

  • • Six simple machines: Lever, pulley, inclined plane, wedge, screw, wheel & axle
  • • MA = Load / Effort; VR = Distance effort / Distance load; η = (MA/VR) × 100%
  • • Machines trade force for distance - same work, easier to do
  • • Real machines always have η < 100% due to friction
  • • Count rope segments in pulley systems to find VR
