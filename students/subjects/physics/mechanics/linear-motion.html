Linear Motion

Understanding motion in a straight line through displacement, velocity, and acceleration

Basic Concepts

Position & Distance

  • • Position: Location relative to reference point
  • • Distance: Length of actual path taken
  • • Distance is scalar (magnitude only)
  • • Position is a vector quantity

Displacement

  • • Vector quantity (has direction)
  • • Change in position
  • • Straight line from start to end
  • • Can be positive or negative

Reference Frame

  • • Origin point (0 position)
  • • Direction convention (+/-)
  • • Choice of coordinates
  • • Motion is relative

Velocity

Average Velocity

  • • Total displacement ÷ total time
  • • v̄ = Δx/Δt
  • • Vector quantity
  • • Units: m/s (SI)

Instantaneous Velocity

  • • Velocity at specific instant
  • • v = lim[Δt→0](Δx/Δt)
  • • Tangent to position curve
  • • Speed = |velocity|

Speed vs Velocity

Speed

  • • Scalar quantity
  • • Total distance ÷ time
  • • Always positive
  • • No direction specified

Velocity

  • • Vector quantity
  • • Displacement ÷ time
  • • Can be positive/negative
  • • Direction specified

Acceleration

Average Acceleration

  • • Change in velocity ÷ time taken
  • • ā = Δv/Δt
  • • Vector quantity
  • • Units: m/s² (SI)

Instantaneous Acceleration

  • • Rate of change of velocity
  • • a = dv/dt
  • • Tangent to velocity curve
  • • Direction can vary

Sign Conventions

Positive Acceleration

  • • Speed increasing in +ve direction
  • • Speed decreasing in -ve direction

Negative Acceleration

  • • Speed decreasing in +ve direction
  • • Speed increasing in -ve direction

Graphical Analysis

Position-Time Graph

  • • Slope = velocity
  • • Curved = changing velocity
  • • Straight = constant velocity
  • • Horizontal = stationary

Velocity-Time Graph

  • • Slope = acceleration
  • • Area = displacement
  • • Straight = constant acceleration
  • • Horizontal = constant velocity

Acceleration-Time Graph

  • • Area = change in velocity
  • • Horizontal = constant acceleration
  • • Zero line = no acceleration
  • • Sign shows direction

Practice Problems

  1. Distance vs Displacement

    A student walks 40 meters east, then 30 meters west. Calculate:

    • Total distance traveled
    • Net displacement

  2. Average Velocity

    A car travels 150 meters east in 10 seconds, then 60 meters west in 5 seconds. Find:

    • Average velocity for each segment
    • Overall average velocity

  3. Acceleration

    A car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate:

    • Average acceleration
    • Distance traveled (assuming constant acceleration)
