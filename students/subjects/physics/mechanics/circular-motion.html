Circular Motion

Understanding motion in a circle and the forces involved

Basic Concepts

Key Terms

  • • Radius (r): Distance from center
  • • Period (T): Time for one revolution
  • • Frequency (f): Revolutions per second
  • • Angular velocity (ω): Rate of angle change

Important Relations

  • • f = 1/T
  • • ω = 2π/T = 2πf
  • • v = 2πr/T = ωr
  • • v = linear velocity

Uniform Circular Motion

Characteristics

Motion Properties

  • • Constant speed
  • • Varying velocity (direction changes)
  • • Constant radius
  • • Constant angular velocity

Key Features

  • • Path is circular
  • • Speed remains constant
  • • Acceleration toward center
  • • Requires centripetal force

Centripetal Acceleration

Formula

  • • a = v²/r
  • • a = ω²r
  • • a = 4π²r/T²
  • • Always points to center

Properties

  • • Causes direction change
  • • Magnitude constant
  • • Direction changes continuously
  • • Required for circular motion

Common Examples

Natural Motion

  • • Moon around Earth
  • • Planets around Sun
  • • Electrons around nucleus

Mechanical Systems

  • • Car turning
  • • Merry-go-round
  • • Fan blades

Laboratory

  • • Centrifuge
  • • Whirling apparatus
  • • Conical pendulum

Forces Providing Centripetal Acceleration

Types of Forces

  • • Tension (in string)
  • • Friction (on road)
  • • Gravity (orbital motion)
  • • Normal force (banked roads)

Force Requirements

  • • F = mv²/r
  • • F = mω²r
  • • Points to center
  • • Must be maintained
