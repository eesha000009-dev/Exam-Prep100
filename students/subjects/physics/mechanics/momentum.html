Momentum & Impulse

Understanding momentum, conservation laws, and impulse in collisions

Linear Momentum

Definition

  • • p = mv (mass × velocity)
  • • Vector quantity
  • • Units: kg⋅m/s
  • • Direction same as velocity

Properties

  • • Conserved in closed systems
  • • Changes with force application
  • • Important in collisions
  • • Depends on reference frame

Conservation of Momentum

Principle

Total momentum of an isolated system remains constant if no external forces act on it.

Mathematical Form

  • • p₁ + p₂ = p₁' + p₂'
  • • Before collision = After collision
  • • Vector sum important
  • • Valid in all directions

Applications

  • • Collisions
  • • Explosions
  • • Rocket propulsion
  • • Nuclear reactions

Impulse

Definition

  • • J = F⋅Δt
  • • J = Δp (change in momentum)
  • • Units: N⋅s
  • • Vector quantity

Applications

  • • Safety features
  • • Sports equipment
  • • Vehicle collisions
  • • Impact force reduction

Types of Collisions

Elastic Collisions

  • • Momentum conserved
  • • Kinetic energy conserved
  • • Perfect rebound
  • • Example: Atomic collisions

Inelastic Collisions

  • • Momentum conserved
  • • Energy lost to heat/sound
  • • Objects separate
  • • Example: Car crashes

Perfectly Inelastic

  • • Momentum conserved
  • • Maximum energy loss
  • • Objects stick together
  • • Example: Clay collision
