Work, Energy & Power

Understanding the relationships between work, energy, and power in mechanical systems

Work

Definition & Formula

  • • W = F·d·cos θ
  • • Units: Joules (N·m)
  • • Scalar quantity
  • • Can be positive or negative

Special Cases

  • • Zero work: Force perpendicular to displacement
  • • Maximum work: Force parallel to displacement
  • • Negative work: Force opposes displacement
  • • Work by gravity: mgh (independent of path)

Energy

Kinetic Energy

  • • KE = ½mv²
  • • Energy of motion
  • • Always positive
  • • Depends on mass and velocity

Potential Energy

  • • Gravitational: PE = mgh
  • • Elastic: PE = ½kx²
  • • Stored energy
  • • Reference point dependent

Conservation of Energy

Law Statement

  • • Total energy remains constant
  • • Energy cannot be created/destroyed
  • • Can be converted between forms
  • • Valid in isolated systems

Applications

  • • Pendulum motion
  • • Roller coasters
  • • Spring oscillations
  • • Free fall

Power

Definition & Formulas

  • • P = W/t (work per time)
  • • P = F·v (force × velocity)
  • • Units: Watts (J/s)
  • • Instantaneous vs. average power

Real-World Examples

  • • Engine power ratings
  • • Electrical appliances
  • • Human power output
  • • Industrial machinery

Problem-Solving Examples

Example 1: Roller Coaster

A roller coaster car starts from rest at height h. Find its velocity at the bottom.

Solution approach:

  1. Initial energy (all potential)
  2. Final energy (all kinetic)
  3. Apply conservation of energy
  4. Solve for velocity

Example 2: Power Calculation

Calculate the power needed to lift a 50kg box 2m in 3 seconds at constant velocity.

Key points:

  • Work against gravity
  • Constant velocity means constant power
  • Use P = W/t

Practice Problems

  1. Spring System

    A 2kg mass is attached to a spring with k = 100 N/m. Find the maximum velocity if compressed 10cm.

  2. Inclined Plane

    Calculate work done pushing a 5kg box up a 30° incline for 4m with friction coefficient 0.2.

  3. Power Output

    An athlete runs upstairs, gaining 10m elevation in 5 seconds. Calculate power output if athlete mass is 70kg.
