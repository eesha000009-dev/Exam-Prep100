Equations of Motion

Understanding uniform acceleration and motion under gravity

Key Assumptions

These equations apply when:

  • • Acceleration is constant
  • • Motion is in a straight line
  • • Air resistance is negligible
  • • No external forces (except gravity in free fall)

Basic Equations of Motion

Variable Definitions

  • v = final velocity (m/s)
  • u = initial velocity (m/s)
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)
  • t = time (s)
  • s = displacement (m)

Four Basic Equations

  • v = u + at
  • s = ut + ½at²
  • v² = u² + 2as
  • s = ½(u + v)t

When to Use Each Equation

First Equation (v = u + at)

  • • Finding final velocity
  • • Known time period
  • • Constant acceleration

Second Equation (s = ut + ½at²)

  • • Finding displacement
  • • Known time period
  • • Initial velocity known

Motion Under Gravity

Free Fall

  • • Acceleration = 9.81 m/s² (g)
  • • Downward is positive
  • • Upward is negative
  • • Same for all masses

Modified Equations

  • • v = u + gt
  • • h = ut - ½gt²
  • • v² = u² - 2gh
  • • h = height, g = 9.81 m/s²

Special Cases

Object Thrown Upward

  • • Initial velocity is positive upward
  • • Maximum height when v = 0
  • • Time up = time down
  • • Speed at start = speed at end

Object Dropped

  • • Initial velocity = 0
  • • s = ½gt²
  • • v = gt
  • • v² = 2gh

Problem-Solving Strategy

  1. List Known Variables

    Write down all given values and identify what you need to find

  2. Choose Correct Equation

    Select equation that contains your unknown and known variables

  3. Check Units

    Convert all values to SI units before calculation

  4. Solve and Check

    Solve algebraically and verify if answer is reasonable

Practice Problems

  1. Free Fall

    A ball is dropped from a height of 45 meters. Calculate:

    • Time taken to reach ground
    • Final velocity on impact

  2. Upward Throw

    A stone is thrown upward with initial velocity 15 m/s. Find:

    • Maximum height reached
    • Total time in air

  3. General Motion

    A car accelerates uniformly from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate:

    • Acceleration
    • Distance traveled
