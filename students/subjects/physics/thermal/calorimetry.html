Calorimetry

Calorimetry uses heat balance: m1 c1 ΔT1 + m2 c2 ΔT2 = 0 (isolated mixing). Specific heat capacity c is key.

Practice

  1. 500 g of water at 20°C receives 10 kJ. Find final temperature (c_water = 4186 J/kg·K).

Solution (brief)

Energy = mcΔT → ΔT = Q/(mc) = 10000 / (0.5×4186) ≈ 4.78 K → Tf ≈ 24.78°C.
