Pressure in Fluids

Pressure = force per unit area. In fluids, pressure increases with depth: p = p0 + ρgh.

Key formula

p = p0 + ρ g h (where ρ is fluid density, g is gravity, h is depth)

Worked example

Find gauge pressure at 2 m depth in freshwater (ρ = 1000 kg/m³). p = ρgh = 1000 × 9.81 × 2 ≈ 19620 Pa.

Practice

  1. Compute absolute pressure at 5 m depth (assume atmospheric pressure = 101325 Pa).
  2. How does pressure change if fluid density doubles?

Solutions (brief)

  1. Gauge p = 1000×9.81×5 = 49050 Pa → absolute ≈ 150375 Pa.
  2. Pressure increase with depth doubles because p = ρgh (directly proportional to ρ).
