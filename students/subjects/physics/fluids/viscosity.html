Viscosity & Flow

Viscosity measures a fluid's resistance to flow. Stoke's law for small spheres: F = 6π η r v.

Key formula

Stoke's drag: F = 6π η r v (η is dynamic viscosity)

Practice

  1. Describe how viscosity affects terminal velocity of a falling sphere.
  2. Using Stoke's law, compute drag on a 1 mm radius sphere moving at 0.1 m/s in a fluid with η = 0.001 Pa·s.

Solutions (brief)

  1. Higher viscosity increases drag, reducing terminal velocity.
  2. F = 6π×0.001×0.001×0.1 ≈ 1.88×10⁻⁶ N.
