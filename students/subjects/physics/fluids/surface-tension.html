Surface Tension

Surface tension arises from cohesive forces between liquid molecules; causes droplets to form and objects to float if they don't break the surface.

Examples

  • Capillary rise in a thin tube: h = 2γ cosθ / (ρ g r).
  • Water striders walking on water due to surface tension.

Practice

  1. Compute capillary rise for water (γ≈0.072 N/m) in a 1 mm radius tube (assume cosθ=1).

Solutions (brief)

  1. h = 2×0.072/(1000×9.81×0.001) ≈ 14.7 mm.
