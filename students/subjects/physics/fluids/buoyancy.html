Buoyancy & Archimedes' Principle

An object immersed in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of displaced fluid: F_b = ρ_fluid V_displaced g.

Worked example

A 0.2 m³ block submerged in water (ρ=1000 kg/m³). Buoyant force = 1000×0.2×9.81 = 1962 N.

Practice

  1. Determine if a 500 kg steel ship displacing 0.5 m³ of water floats or sinks.
  2. Calculate apparent weight of a 10 N object submerged in a fluid where buoyant force is 3 N.

Solutions (brief)

  1. Weight = mg = 500×9.81 = 4905 N. Buoyant if displaced-water weight ≥4905 N; 0.5 m³ displaces 0.5×1000×9.81 = 4905 N → neutrally floating (edge case).
  2. Apparent weight = actual weight − buoyant = 10 − 3 = 7 N.
