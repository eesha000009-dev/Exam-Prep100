Bernoulli's Principle

For steady, incompressible, non-viscous flow: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant along a streamline.

Worked example

Flow speed increases through a constriction; pressure drops — basic lift explanation on airfoils (qualitative).

Practice

  1. Using Bernoulli, compute pressure difference if speed increases from 2 m/s to 6 m/s in air (ρ≈1.225 kg/m³).

Solutions (brief)

  1. ΔP = ½ρ(v2² − v1²) = 0.5×1.225×(36 − 4) ≈ 19.15 Pa.
