Measurement in Physics

Understanding precision, accuracy, and the proper use of measuring instruments

Basics of Measurement

Measurement is the process of finding the magnitude of a physical quantity in terms of a standard unit. Key concepts include:

Accuracy

How close a measured value is to the true value. High accuracy means measurements are near the accepted value.

Precision

How close repeated measurements are to each other. High precision means consistent results.

Common Measuring Instruments

Vernier Caliper

Used for measuring:

  • Length
  • Internal diameter
  • External diameter
  • Depth

Key Features:

  • Least count: 0.1 mm or 0.01 mm
  • Main scale in mm
  • Vernier scale for precision

Micrometer Screw Gauge

Used for measuring:

  • Very small lengths
  • Thickness of wires
  • Thickness of sheets

Key Features:

  • Least count: 0.01 mm
  • Pitch: 0.5 mm
  • 100 divisions on circular scale

Other Important Instruments

Measuring Cylinder

  • Measures volume of liquids
  • Available in different capacities
  • Read at eye level for accuracy

Physical Balance

  • Measures mass accurately
  • Uses standard weights
  • Requires level surface

Errors in Measurement

Systematic Errors

  • • Zero error in instruments
  • • Calibration errors
  • • Personal bias
  • • Can be corrected

Random Errors

  • • Unpredictable variations
  • • Environmental factors
  • • Human limitations
  • • Reduced by averaging

Gross Errors

  • • Careless mistakes
  • • Wrong reading noted
  • • Calculation mistakes
  • • Should be discarded

Significant Figures

Significant figures indicate the precision of a measurement. Rules for counting significant figures:

Count as Significant

  • • All non-zero digits
  • • Zeros between non-zero digits
  • • Trailing zeros after decimal

Examples

  • • 1.234 → 4 sig figs
  • • 100.00 → 5 sig figs
  • • 0.00234 → 3 sig figs
