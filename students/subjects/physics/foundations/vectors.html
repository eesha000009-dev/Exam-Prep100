Scalars and Vectors

Understanding quantities that have magnitude only vs. those with both magnitude and direction

Basic Definitions

Scalar Quantities

Quantities with magnitude only.

Examples:

  • Mass: 5 kg
  • Temperature: 20°C
  • Time: 10 seconds
  • Speed: 30 m/s
  • Distance: 100 m

Vector Quantities

Quantities with both magnitude and direction.

Examples:

  • Velocity: 30 m/s east
  • Force: 10 N upward
  • Displacement: 5 m north
  • Acceleration: 9.8 m/s² down
  • Momentum: 15 kg⋅m/s west

Vector Representation

Ways to Represent Vectors

  • 1. Arrow notation: →
  • 2. Bold letters: F
  • 3. Letter with arrow: v̅
  • 4. Magnitude and direction: 10 N at 30°
  • 5. Component form: (3i + 4j) units

Important Terms

  • • Initial point (tail)
  • • Terminal point (head)
  • • Direction angle
  • • Components (x and y)
  • • Unit vectors (i, j, k)

Vector Operations

Addition and Subtraction

Methods of Addition

  • 1. Triangle method
  • 2. Parallelogram method
  • 3. Polygon method
  • 4. Component method

Key Points

  • • Vector addition is commutative
  • • Subtraction = adding negative vector
  • • Resultant depends on angle between vectors

Vector Multiplication

Scalar (Dot) Product

  • • Result is a scalar
  • • A⋅B = |A||B|cosθ
  • • Used for work calculations

Vector (Cross) Product

  • • Result is a vector
  • • |A×B| = |A||B|sinθ
  • • Direction by right-hand rule

Resolution of Vectors

Component Method

  • • x-component = |F|cosθ
  • • y-component = |F|sinθ
  • • Magnitude = √(x² + y²)
  • • Direction = tan⁻¹(y/x)

Applications

  • • Force analysis
  • • Velocity components
  • • Projectile motion
  • • Equilibrium problems

Practice Problems

  1. Vector Addition:

    Two forces act on a point: 3 N east and 4 N north. Find the magnitude and direction of the resultant force.

    Hint: Use the Pythagorean theorem and tan⁻¹

    A force of 10 N acts at 60° to the horizontal. Find its horizontal and vertical components.

    Hint: Use F cosθ and F sinθ
