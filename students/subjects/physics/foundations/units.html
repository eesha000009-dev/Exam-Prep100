Unit Conversion

Conversion Method

  1. Write the given quantity with its unit
  2. Write the conversion factor as a fraction
  3. Multiply and cancel out units
  4. Calculate the final answer

Common Conversions

  • 1 km = 1000 m
  • 1 hour = 3600 seconds
  • 1 kg = 1000 g
  • 1 N = 1 kg⋅m/s²

Dimensional Analysis

Basic Dimensions

Fundamental Dimensions:

  • • Length: [L]
  • • Mass: [M]
  • • Time: [T]
  • • Temperature: [θ]

Examples of Derived Dimensions:

  • • Velocity: [LT⁻¹]
  • • Force: [MLT⁻²]
  • • Energy: [ML²T⁻²]
  • • Pressure: [ML⁻¹T⁻²]

Uses of Dimensional Analysis

Checking Equations

  • • Verify equation correctness
  • • Find missing constants
  • • Identify errors

Deriving Relations

  • • Find possible relationships
  • • Determine dependencies
  • • Simplify problems

Steps in Dimensional Analysis

  1. 1. Write Dimensional Formula

    Express each quantity in terms of fundamental dimensions [M], [L], [T]

  2. 2. Check Dimensional Homogeneity

    Both sides of equation must have same dimensions

  3. 3. Apply Dimensional Methods

    Use methods like Rayleigh's method for complex problems

Practice Problems

  1. Unit Conversion:

    Convert 72 kilometers per hour to meters per second.

    Hint: Use 1 km = 1000 m and 1 hr = 3600 s

  2. Dimensional Check:

    Find the dimensions of pressure (P = F/A) using fundamental dimensions.

    Hint: Force dimensions are [MLT⁻²] and area is [L²]

  3. Dimensional Analysis:

    The period (T) of a simple pendulum depends on length (l) and acceleration due to gravity (g). Find the relationship.

    Hint: Use T = k√(l/g) where k is dimensionless

Common Errors to Avoid

In Unit Conversion

  • • Forgetting to convert all units
  • • Using wrong conversion factors
  • • Not canceling units properly

In Dimensional Analysis

  • • Missing fundamental dimensions
  • • Incorrect dimensional equations
  • • Not checking homogeneity
