Electric Charges & Electrostatics

Basic properties of electric charge, Coulomb's law, electric field and potential at an introductory level.

Key concepts

  • Charge is quantized and conserved.
  • Like charges repel, unlike charges attract.
  • Coulomb's law: force between point charges.
    F = k * |q1 * q2| / r²
  • Electric field E = F/q (vector field) and electric potential V = work per unit charge.

Worked example

Two charges +2 μC and -3 μC are 0.5 m apart. Find the magnitude of the force between them (use k = 9×10^9 N·m²/C²).

  1. Convert microcoulombs to coulombs: q1 = 2e-6 C, q2 = -3e-6 C.
  2. Apply Coulomb's law: F = k|q1q2|/r² = 9e9 * (6e-12) / 0.25 = 9e9 * 2.4e-11 = 0.216 N.
  3. Direction: attractive (opposite signs).

Practice questions

  1. Calculate the electric field at 0.2 m from a point charge of +5 μC.
  2. Two like charges of 10 μC each are placed 1 m apart. Calculate the force.
  3. Find the potential difference between two points 0.1 m and 0.3 m from a +1 μC charge.

Solutions (brief)

  1. E = kq/r² = 9e9 * 5e-6 / 0.04 = 1.125e6 N/C.
  2. F = kq²/r² = 9e9 * (1e-10) / 1 = 9e-1 = 0.9 N.
  3. ΔV = kq(1/r1 - 1/r2) = 9e9*1e-6*(10 - 3.333) ≈ 60 000 V (approx.).
