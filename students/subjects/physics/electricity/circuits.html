Current Electricity & Circuits

Ohm's law, series and parallel circuits, and common measuring instruments.

Ohm's Law

Ohm's law relates voltage (V), current (I) and resistance (R): V = I R. It holds for many conductors over certain ranges.

Series and Parallel Circuits

Series: resistances add (R_total = R1 + R2 + ...), current is same through each component. Parallel: reciprocals add (1/R_total = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...), voltage same across each branch.

Measuring Instruments

Ammeters measure current and are placed in series. Voltmeters measure voltage and are placed in parallel. Both have internal resistances that affect measurements.

Simple Circuit Analysis Example

Find current through a 2 Ω resistor when connected in series with a 3 Ω resistor across a 10 V battery: I = V / (R_total) = 10 / (2+3) = 2 A.

Practical Notes

  • Kirchhoff's laws for complex circuits (loop and junction rules).
  • Power dissipation in resistors: P = I^2 R = V^2 / R.
  • Care when measuring: choose appropriate meter ranges and connections.
