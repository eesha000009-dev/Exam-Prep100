Current Electricity & Circuits

Ohm's law, series and parallel circuits, Kirchhoff's rules and practical measurements.

Ohm's law & basics

V = IR relates potential difference, current and resistance. Power in circuits: P = VI = I²R = V²/R.

Series & Parallel

  • Series: resistances add (R = R1 + R2 + ...). Current is same through each element.
  • Parallel: reciprocals add (1/R = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...). Voltage across each branch is the same.

Worked problem

Find equivalent resistance of 10 Ω and 20 Ω in parallel, then in series with 5 Ω.

R_parallel = 1 / (1/10 + 1/20) = 1/(0.15) = 6.666... Ω. Total R = 6.666... + 5 = 11.666... Ω.

Practical notes

Using ammeters and voltmeters: ammeters in series, voltmeters in parallel; internal resistance of meters affects readings.

Practice

  1. Calculate current through a 12 V battery connected to 4 Ω total resistance.
  2. A 60 W bulb operates at 240 V. Compute its resistance.
  3. Find the equivalent resistance of three resistors 5 Ω, 10 Ω and 20 Ω where 10 and 20 are in parallel, then series with 5 Ω.

Solutions (brief)

  1. I = V/R = 12/4 = 3 A.
  2. R = V²/P = 240²/60 = 960 Ω.
  3. R_parallel = 1/(1/10 + 1/20) = 6.666... Ω → Total = 6.666... + 5 = 11.666... Ω.
