Electrical Energy & Power

Energy transfer and power in electrical systems; domestic and industrial considerations.

Key formulas

P = VI, P = I²R, P = V²/R. Energy E = Pt (power × time).

Example

A heater draws 10 A at 230 V. Compute power and energy used in 3 hours.

P = VI = 2300 W. Energy = 2.3 kW × 3 h = 6.9 kWh.

Practice

  1. Calculate monthly energy cost for a 60 W bulb used 5 hours/day at $0.12/kWh.
  2. A device rated 1500 W runs 2 hours daily. Compute monthly energy (30 days) and cost at $0.15/kWh.

Solutions (brief)

  1. Energy/day = 0.06 kW × 5 h = 0.3 kWh → Month = 9 kWh → Cost = 9 × 0.12 = $1.08.
  2. Energy/day = 1.5 kW × 2 h = 3 kWh → Month = 90 kWh → Cost = 90 × 0.15 = $13.50.
