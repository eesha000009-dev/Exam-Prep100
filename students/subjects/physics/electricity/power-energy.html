Electrical Energy & Power

How circuits consume power and how energy is calculated over time.

Electrical Power

Instantaneous electrical power delivered to a component is P = V I. Using Ohm's law we get P = I^2 R = V^2 / R.

Energy and Units

Energy consumed over time t is E = P t. Common units: watt (W) for power, joule (J) for energy; electrical energy billed in kWh (kilowatt-hour).

Worked Example

A 60 W lamp runs for 5 hours. Energy used = 60 W × 5 h = 300 Wh = 0.3 kWh.

Practical Considerations

  • Efficiency: not all input energy is delivered usefully (heat losses in components).
  • Power ratings on components indicate safe operating limits.
  • Measuring energy in circuits uses wattmeters or integrating measured power over time.
