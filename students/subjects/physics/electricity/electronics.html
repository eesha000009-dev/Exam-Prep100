Solutions (brief)

  1. Diode conducts when forward biased (barrier lowered) and blocks when reverse biased (barrier high); avalanche breakdown if voltage too large.
  2. Transistor in common-emitter amplifies: small base-emitter voltage changes large collector current, producing larger voltage swing across a load resistor.
