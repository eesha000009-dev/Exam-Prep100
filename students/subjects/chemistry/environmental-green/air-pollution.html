1 What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution is the introduction of harmful substances into the atmosphere, causing damage to living organisms and the environment.

Why is Air Pollution a Problem?

  • • Causes respiratory diseases in humans and animals
  • • Damages crops and reduces agricultural yields
  • • Contributes to climate change and global warming
  • • Causes acid rain that damages buildings and ecosystems
  • • Depletes the ozone layer

Primary Pollutants

Released directly from sources:

  • • Carbon monoxide (CO)
  • • Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ)
  • • Sulfur dioxide (SO₂)
  • • Particulate matter (PM)

Secondary Pollutants

Formed by chemical reactions:

  • • Ozone (O₃) at ground level
  • • Acid rain (H₂SO₄, HNO₃)
  • • Photochemical smog
  • • Secondary particulates

2 Major Air Pollutants

Pollutant Formula Sources Effects
Carbon Monoxide CO Vehicle exhaust, incomplete combustion Reduces oxygen transport in blood; fatal at high levels
Sulfur Dioxide SO₂ Burning fossil fuels, volcanic eruptions Acid rain, respiratory problems
Nitrogen Oxides NOₓ Vehicle engines, power plants Smog, acid rain, ozone formation
Particulate Matter PM Dust, smoke, construction, vehicles Lung diseases, reduced visibility
Volatile Organic Compounds VOCs Paints, solvents, fuel evaporation Smog formation, health effects
Ground-level Ozone O₃ NOₓ + VOCs in sunlight Respiratory irritation, crop damage

3 Acid Rain

Acid rain is rain with a pH below 5.6, caused by sulfur and nitrogen oxides reacting with water in the atmosphere.

Formation

From Sulfur Dioxide:

SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₃
2H₂SO₃ + O₂ → 2H₂SO₄

From Nitrogen Oxides:

2NO₂ + H₂O → HNO₂ + HNO₃
2NO₂ + H₂O + ½O₂ → 2HNO₃

Effects

  • Lakes & Rivers: Lowers pH, kills fish and aquatic life
  • Forests: Damages leaves, leaches nutrients from soil
  • Buildings: Erodes limestone and marble structures
  • Human Health: Respiratory problems

Effect on Buildings

Acid rain reacts with calcium carbonate in limestone and marble:

CaCO₃ + H₂SO₄ → CaSO₄ + H₂O + CO₂

This causes statues and buildings to erode and crumble over time.

4 Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming

The greenhouse effect is a natural process where certain gases trap heat in the atmosphere. Human activities have enhanced this effect, causing global warming.

Natural Greenhouse Effect

  • • Essential for life on Earth
  • • Keeps Earth ~33°C warmer
  • • Without it, Earth would be frozen
  • • Caused by water vapor, CO₂, CH₄ naturally

Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

  • • Caused by human activities
  • • Rising global temperatures
  • • Melting ice caps, rising sea levels
  • • Extreme weather events

Main Greenhouse Gases

CO₂

Burning fossil fuels, deforestation

CH₄

Livestock, rice paddies, landfills

N₂O

Fertilizers, industrial processes

CFCs

Refrigerants, aerosols

5 Photochemical Smog

Photochemical smog is a type of air pollution formed when sunlight reacts with nitrogen oxides and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) in the atmosphere.

Formation Process

  1. 1NO₂ (from vehicles) absorbs UV light → NO + O·
  2. 2O· + O₂ → O₃ (ozone)
  3. 3VOCs + NOₓ + sunlight → Peroxyacyl nitrates (PANs)
  4. 4Mixture of O₃, PANs, NOₓ forms brown haze (smog)

Health Effects

  • • Eye and throat irritation
  • • Respiratory problems
  • • Aggravates asthma
  • • Reduced lung function

Conditions for Formation

  • • High traffic density
  • • Strong sunlight (UV radiation)
  • • Temperature inversion
  • • Low wind speed

6 Air Pollution Control Methods

Vehicle Emissions

  • • Catalytic converters: Convert CO, NOₓ to CO₂, N₂
  • • Lean-burn engines: More efficient combustion
  • • Electric vehicles: Zero tailpipe emissions
  • • Improved fuel quality: Low-sulfur fuels

Industrial Controls

  • • Scrubbers: Remove SO₂ using lime slurry
  • • Electrostatic precipitators: Remove particulates
  • • Fabric filters: Capture fine particles
  • • Flue gas desulfurization: Remove sulfur compounds

Alternative Energy

  • • Solar and wind power
  • • Hydroelectric power
  • • Nuclear energy
  • • Biofuels from sustainable sources

Regulations

  • • Emission standards for vehicles
  • • Industrial emission limits
  • • Air quality monitoring
  • • Carbon taxes and trading schemes

Practice Questions

1. Write equations to show how sulfur dioxide forms acid rain.

Show Answer
Step 1: SO₂ dissolves in water
SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₃ (sulfurous acid)

Step 2: Oxidation to sulfuric acid
2H₂SO₃ + O₂ → 2H₂SO₄ (sulfuric acid)

2. Explain how a catalytic converter reduces vehicle emissions.

Show Answer
A catalytic converter contains platinum, palladium, and rhodium catalysts that:
  • Oxidation reactions: 2CO + O₂ → 2CO₂ and CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
  • Reduction reactions: 2NO → N₂ + O₂ and 2NO₂ → N₂ + 2O₂
These convert harmful gases (CO, NOₓ, hydrocarbons) into less harmful products (CO₂, N₂, H₂O).

3. Why is ground-level ozone considered a pollutant while stratospheric ozone is beneficial?

Show Answer
Stratospheric ozone (ozone layer): Located 15-35 km up, it absorbs harmful UV radiation from the sun, protecting living organisms from DNA damage.

Ground-level ozone: A component of photochemical smog that causes respiratory problems, eye irritation, and damages crops. It's harmful because we breathe it directly.

4. Describe how a flue gas desulfurization (FGD) system works.

Show Answer
FGD systems (scrubbers) remove SO₂ from power plant emissions:
  1. Flue gas containing SO₂ passes through a scrubber
  2. Spray of limestone slurry (CaCO₃) or lime (CaO) reacts with SO₂
  3. CaCO₃ + SO₂ → CaSO₃ + CO₂
  4. CaSO₃ is oxidized to CaSO₄ (gypsum)
  5. Gypsum can be sold for construction materials
This removes 90-97% of SO₂ from emissions.

Key Takeaways

  • • Primary pollutants: CO, SO₂, NOₓ, particulates; Secondary: O₃, acid rain, smog
  • • Acid rain: SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₄; NOₓ + H₂O → HNO₃ (pH < 5.6)
  • • Greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, CFCs trap heat and cause global warming
  • • Photochemical smog: NOₓ + VOCs + sunlight → O₃ + PANs
  • • Controls: Catalytic converters, scrubbers, electrostatic precipitators, alternative energy
