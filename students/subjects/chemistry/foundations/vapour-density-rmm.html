Vapour Density & Relative Molecular Mass

Understanding the relationship between vapour density and molecular mass, and how to calculate them.

Learning Objectives

  • • Define vapour density and relative molecular mass
  • • State the relationship between VD and RMM
  • • Calculate RMM from vapour density
  • • Determine molecular formula from empirical formula and VD
  • • Solve problems involving vapour density

1 Definitions

Vapour Density (VD)

The vapour density of a gas is the ratio of the mass of a certain volume of the gas to the mass of an equal volume of hydrogen gas under the same conditions of temperature and pressure.

VD = Mass of V volume of gas / Mass of V volume of H₂

Relative Molecular Mass (Mᵣ)

The relative molecular mass is the mass of one molecule of a substance compared to 1/12th the mass of a carbon-12 atom. It has no units.

Mᵣ = Sum of relative atomic masses in formula

Important Note

Vapour density is only defined for gases or substances that can be vaporized. It is a ratio and has no units.

2 The Key Relationship

The Relationship

Mᵣ = 2 × VD

or equivalently: VD = Mᵣ / 2

Why is this relationship true?

  1. 1 By Avogadro's Law, equal volumes of gases contain equal numbers of molecules
  2. 2 VD = Mass of n molecules of gas / Mass of n molecules of H₂
  3. 3 VD = Mass of 1 molecule / Mass of 1 H₂ molecule
  4. 4 VD = Mᵣ / 2 (since H₂ has Mᵣ = 2)

Remember

Hydrogen (H₂) has a relative molecular mass of 2. This is why we multiply VD by 2 to get Mᵣ.

3 Worked Examples

A gas has vapour density of 16. Calculate its relative molecular mass.

Formula: Mᵣ = 2 × VD

Substitute: Mᵣ = 2 × 16

Answer: Mᵣ = 32 (This is O₂)

Calculate the vapour density of carbon dioxide (CO₂).

Find Mᵣ: Mᵣ(CO₂) = 12 + (2×16) = 44

Formula: VD = Mᵣ / 2

Substitute: VD = 44 / 2 = 22

Answer: VD = 22

A compound has empirical formula CH₂O and vapour density 30. Find its molecular formula.

Step 1: Find Mᵣ

Mᵣ = 2 × VD = 2 × 30 = 60

Step 2: Find empirical formula mass

CH₂O = 12 + (2×1) + 16 = 30

Step 3: Find n

n = Mᵣ / empirical mass = 60 / 30 = 2

Molecular formula = (CH₂O)₂ = C₂H₄O₂

This is ethanoic acid (acetic acid)

Practice Problems

1. A gas has vapour density of 8. Calculate its relative molecular mass and identify the gas.

Show Solution

Mᵣ = 2 × VD = 2 × 8 = 16

A gas with Mᵣ = 16 is methane (CH₄)

2. Calculate the vapour density of SO₂.

Show Solution

Mᵣ(SO₂) = 32 + (2×16) = 64

VD = Mᵣ / 2 = 64 / 2 = 32

3. A hydrocarbon has empirical formula CH and vapour density 39. Find its molecular formula.

Show Solution

Mᵣ = 2 × 39 = 78

Empirical mass = 12 + 1 = 13

n = 78 / 13 = 6

Molecular formula = C₆H₆ (benzene)

4. State the relationship between vapour density and relative molecular mass.

Show Solution

Mᵣ = 2 × VD or VD = Mᵣ / 2

The relative molecular mass is twice the vapour density.

5. Why is VD compared to hydrogen and not oxygen?

Show Solution

Hydrogen is the lightest gas (Mᵣ = 2), making calculations simple. The factor of 2 in the relationship Mᵣ = 2 × VD comes from the molecular mass of H₂ being 2.

Key Takeaways for WAEC/JAMB/NECO

  • • VD = Mᵣ / 2 and Mᵣ = 2 × VD
  • • Vapour density is defined relative to hydrogen
  • • Hydrogen has Mᵣ = 2, so VD = 1
  • • Use VD to find Mᵣ, then determine molecular formula
  • • VD = (mass of gas) / (mass of equal volume of H₂)
  • • Remember: VD is a ratio, has no units
