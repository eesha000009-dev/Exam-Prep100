What is an Atom?

An atom is the smallest particle of an element that can take part in a chemical reaction. The word "atom" comes from the Greek word "atomos" meaning indivisible. However, we now know that atoms are made up of even smaller particles called subatomic particles.

Atoms are incredibly small! A typical atom has a diameter of about 0.1 to 0.5 nanometers (1 nm = 10⁻⁹ m). If you could line up 10 million atoms side by side, they would only measure about 1 millimeter!

Subatomic Particles

Atoms consist of three main subatomic particles:

Particle Symbol Relative Mass Relative Charge Location
Proton p 1 +1 Nucleus
Neutron n 1 0 Nucleus
Electron e⁻ 1/1840 (≈ 0.0005) −1 Electron shells/orbitals

Key Points to Remember

  • The nucleus contains protons and neutrons, making up most of the atom's mass
  • Electrons orbit the nucleus in electron shells (energy levels)
  • An atom is electrically neutral: number of protons = number of electrons
  • Most of an atom is empty space!

Atomic Number and Mass Number

Atomic Number (Z)

The atomic number is the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom. It identifies the element and determines its position in the periodic table.

Atomic Number (Z) = Number of Protons

Mass Number (A)

The mass number is the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom.

Mass Number (A) = Protons + Neutrons

Nuclear Notation

Mass Number (A) 23
Atomic Number (Z) 11
Na → Sodium atom

This notation tells us: Sodium has 11 protons, 11 electrons, and 23 - 11 = 12 neutrons

Isotopes

Isotopes are atoms of the same element that have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons. This means they have the same atomic number but different mass numbers.

Relative Atomic Mass (Aᵣ)

The relative atomic mass is the weighted average mass of all naturally occurring isotopes of an element, relative to ¹²C = 12 exactly.

Aᵣ = Σ (isotope mass × abundance) / 100

📝 Worked Example

Chlorine has two isotopes: ³⁵Cl (75% abundance) and ³⁷Cl (25% abundance). Calculate the relative atomic mass of chlorine.

Solution:

Aᵣ = (35 × 75 + 37 × 25) / 100

Aᵣ = (2625 + 925) / 100

Aᵣ = 3550 / 100

Aᵣ = 35.5

Historical Development of Atomic Models

Dalton proposed that all matter is made of tiny, solid, indestructible spheres called atoms. He suggested that atoms of the same element are identical and atoms of different elements have different masses.

  • Atoms are solid, indivisible spheres
  • All atoms of an element are identical
  • Atoms combine in simple whole number ratios

After discovering the electron, Thomson proposed that atoms are positive spheres with negative electrons embedded throughout, like plums in a pudding.

  • Atoms contain smaller particles (electrons)
  • Atoms are divisible
  • Positive charge spread throughout the atom

Through his famous gold foil experiment, Rutherford discovered that atoms have a small, dense, positively charged nucleus with electrons orbiting around it.

  • Most of the atom is empty space
  • Positive charge concentrated in tiny nucleus
  • Electrons orbit the nucleus

Bohr proposed that electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed energy levels (shells) and can jump between levels by absorbing or emitting energy.

  • Electrons in fixed orbits (energy levels)
  • Each orbit has specific energy
  • Electrons can jump between levels

Practice Problems

1. An atom has 17 protons and 18 neutrons. What is its atomic number and mass number?

Atomic number = 17 (number of protons)

Mass number = 17 + 18 = 35

This is a chlorine-35 atom.

2. Write the electron configuration for an atom with atomic number 15 (Phosphorus).

Phosphorus has 15 electrons.

Electron configuration: 2, 8, 5

First shell: 2, Second shell: 8, Third shell: 5

3. Copper has two isotopes: ⁶³Cu (69.2%) and ⁶⁵Cu (30.8%). Calculate the relative atomic mass.

Aᵣ = (63 × 69.2 + 65 × 30.8) / 100

Aᵣ = (4359.6 + 2002) / 100

Aᵣ = 6361.6 / 100

Aᵣ = 63.6

4. An ion has 12 protons, 14 neutrons, and 10 electrons. What is the symbol and charge of this ion?

Atomic number = 12 → This is Magnesium (Mg)

Protons = 12 (+12 charge)

Electrons = 10 (-10 charge)

Net charge = +12 + (-10) = +2

Symbol: Mg²⁺ (Magnesium ion)

Real-World Applications

Carbon Dating

The radioactive isotope Carbon-14 is used to determine the age of ancient artifacts and fossils. This technique revolutionized archaeology!

Medical Imaging

Radioactive isotopes like Technetium-99m are used in medical scans to diagnose diseases and study organ function.

Nuclear Power

Uranium-235 undergoes nuclear fission to generate electricity in nuclear power plants, providing clean energy.

Smoke Detectors

Americium-241, a radioactive isotope, is used in household smoke detectors to detect smoke particles.
