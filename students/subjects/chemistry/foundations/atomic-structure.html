Atomic Structure

Understanding the structure of atoms, subatomic particles, isotopes, and electron configuration.

Learning Objectives

  • • Describe the structure of the atom
  • • Identify locations and properties of protons, neutrons, and electrons
  • • Define atomic number and mass number
  • • Explain isotopes and calculate relative atomic mass
  • • Write electron configurations for simple atoms

1 What is an Atom?

An atom is the smallest particle of an element that can take part in a chemical reaction. The word "atom" comes from the Greek word "atomos" meaning indivisible.

Atoms are incredibly small! A typical atom has a diameter of about 0.1 to 0.5 nanometers. If you could line up 10 million atoms side by side, they would only measure about 1 millimeter!

2 Subatomic Particles

Atoms consist of three main subatomic particles:

Particle Symbol Relative Mass Relative Charge Location
Proton p 1 +1 Nucleus
Neutron n 1 0 Nucleus
Electron e⁻ 1/1840 ≈ 0 −1 Electron shells

Key Points

  • • The nucleus contains protons and neutrons (most of the atom's mass)
  • • Electrons orbit the nucleus in electron shells (energy levels)
  • • An atom is neutral: number of protons = number of electrons
  • • Most of an atom is empty space!

3 Atomic Number and Mass Number

Atomic Number (Z)

The number of protons in the nucleus. It identifies the element.

Z = Number of Protons

Mass Number (A)

The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.

A = Protons + Neutrons

Nuclear Notation

A Z
23
11
Na → Sodium atom

Sodium has 11 protons, 11 electrons, and 23 − 11 = 12 neutrons

An atom has atomic number 17 and mass number 35. How many protons, neutrons, and electrons does it have?

Step 1: Find protons

Protons = Atomic number = 17

Step 2: Find electrons (atom is neutral)

Electrons = Protons = 17

Step 3: Find neutrons

Neutrons = Mass number − Atomic number

Neutrons = 35 − 17 = 18

Answer: 17 protons, 17 electrons, 18 neutrons (Chlorine-35)

Relative Atomic Mass (Aᵣ)

The weighted average mass of all naturally occurring isotopes:

Aᵣ = Σ (isotope mass × % abundance) / 100

Chlorine has two isotopes: ³⁵Cl (75%) and ³⁷Cl (25%). Calculate the relative atomic mass.

Formula: Aᵣ = Σ (mass × %)/100

Substitute:

Aᵣ = (35 × 75 + 37 × 25) / 100

Aᵣ = (2625 + 925) / 100

Aᵣ = 3550 / 100

Answer: Aᵣ = 35.5

5 Electron Configuration

Electrons are arranged in energy levels (shells) around the nucleus. The maximum electrons in each shell follow the formula 2n².

6 Historical Development of Atomic Models

Dalton proposed that all matter is made of tiny, solid, indestructible spheres called atoms.

  • • Atoms are solid, indivisible spheres
  • • All atoms of an element are identical
  • • Atoms combine in simple whole number ratios

After discovering the electron, Thomson proposed atoms are positive spheres with negative electrons embedded throughout.

  • • Atoms contain smaller particles (electrons)
  • • Atoms are divisible
  • • Positive charge spread throughout

Through his gold foil experiment, Rutherford discovered atoms have a small, dense, positively charged nucleus.

  • • Most of the atom is empty space
  • • Positive charge concentrated in tiny nucleus
  • • Electrons orbit the nucleus

Bohr proposed that electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed energy levels and can jump between levels by absorbing or emitting energy.

  • • Electrons in fixed orbits (energy levels)
  • • Each orbit has specific energy
  • • Electrons can jump between levels

Practice Problems

1. An atom has 17 protons and 18 neutrons. What is its atomic number and mass number?

Show Solution

Atomic number = 17 (number of protons)

Mass number = 17 + 18 = 35

This is chlorine-35.

2. Write the electron configuration for phosphorus (Z = 15).

Show Solution

Phosphorus has 15 electrons.

Configuration: 2, 8, 5

First shell: 2, Second shell: 8, Third shell: 5

3. An ion has 12 protons, 14 neutrons, and 10 electrons. What is its symbol and charge?

Show Solution

Z = 12 → Magnesium (Mg)

Charge = +12 + (-10) = +2

Mg²⁺ (Magnesium ion)

4. Copper has isotopes ⁶³Cu (69.2%) and ⁶⁵Cu (30.8%). Calculate Aᵣ.

Show Solution

Aᵣ = (63 × 69.2 + 65 × 30.8) / 100

Aᵣ = (4359.6 + 2002) / 100 = 63.6

Aᵣ = 63.6
