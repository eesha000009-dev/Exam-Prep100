1 Key Equations and Constants

Charge Equation

The total charge transferred during electrolysis is:

Q = I × t

Where: Q = charge (Coulombs), I = current (Amperes), t = time (seconds)

Faraday's Constant

F = 96,500 C/mol

The charge carried by 1 mole of electrons

Molar Gas Volume

Vm = 24 dm³/mol

Volume of 1 mole of gas at RTP (25°C, 1 atm)

Key Relationship

Moles of electrons = Q / F = (I × t) / 96,500

This is the foundation for all electrolysis calculations!

2 Faraday's Laws of Electrolysis

First Law

The mass of substance deposited/dissolved is directly proportional to the quantity of electricity passed.

m ∝ Q

m = (I × t × Ar) / (n × F)

n = number of electrons transferred per ion

Second Law

The masses of different substances deposited by the same quantity of electricity are proportional to their equivalent weights.

m ∝ M/n

Equivalent weight = Ar / n

M = molar mass, n = charge on ion

Common Ion Charges

Ag⁺

n = 1

Cu⁺

n = 1

Cu²⁺

n = 2

Pb²⁺

n = 2

Al³⁺

n = 3

Fe³⁺

n = 3

3 Step-by-Step Calculation Method

General Steps

  1. 1Write the balanced half-equation
  2. 2Calculate charge: Q = I × t
  3. 3Calculate moles of electrons: moles e⁻ = Q / F
  4. 4Use the mole ratio from half-equation
  5. 5Calculate mass or volume as required

For Mass

Mass = moles × Mr

For Gas Volume

Volume = moles × 24 dm³ (at RTP)

4 Worked Example: Calculating Mass

Problem

Calculate the mass of copper deposited when a current of 2.5 A flows through CuSO₄ solution for 30 minutes.

Step 1: Write the half-equation

Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu

Ratio: 2 mol e⁻ : 1 mol Cu

Step 2: Calculate charge

Q = I × t = 2.5 A × (30 × 60) s

Q = 2.5 × 1800 = 4500 C

Step 3: Calculate moles of electrons

Moles e⁻ = Q / F = 4500 / 96500

Moles e⁻ = 0.0466 mol

Step 4: Calculate moles of Cu

From half-equation: 2 mol e⁻ → 1 mol Cu

Moles Cu = 0.0466 / 2 = 0.0233 mol

Step 5: Calculate mass

Mass Cu = moles × Ar = 0.0233 × 63.5

Mass Cu = 1.48 g

5 Worked Example: Calculating Gas Volume

Problem

Calculate the volume of oxygen produced at the anode when a current of 0.5 A flows through dilute H₂SO₄ for 1 hour.

Step 1: Write the half-equation

4OH⁻ → O₂ + 2H₂O + 4e⁻

Ratio: 4 mol e⁻ : 1 mol O₂

Step 2: Calculate charge

Q = I × t = 0.5 A × (1 × 60 × 60) s

Q = 0.5 × 3600 = 1800 C

Step 3: Calculate moles of electrons

Moles e⁻ = Q / F = 1800 / 96500

Moles e⁻ = 0.0187 mol

Step 4: Calculate moles of O₂

From half-equation: 4 mol e⁻ → 1 mol O₂

Moles O₂ = 0.0187 / 4 = 0.00467 mol

Step 5: Calculate volume

Volume O₂ = moles × 24 dm³

Volume O₂ = 0.00467 × 24

Volume O₂ = 0.112 dm³ = 112 cm³

6 Worked Example: Finding Current or Time

Problem

What current is needed to deposit 5.4 g of silver from AgNO₃ solution in 2 hours?

Step 1: Write the half-equation

Ag⁺ + e⁻ → Ag

Ratio: 1 mol e⁻ : 1 mol Ag

Step 2: Calculate moles of Ag

Moles Ag = mass / Ar = 5.4 / 108

Moles Ag = 0.05 mol

Step 3: Calculate moles of electrons

From half-equation: 1 mol Ag requires 1 mol e⁻

Moles e⁻ = 0.05 mol

Step 4: Calculate charge

Q = moles e⁻ × F = 0.05 × 96500

Q = 4825 C

Step 5: Calculate current

Q = I × t → I = Q / t

t = 2 hours = 2 × 60 × 60 = 7200 s

I = 4825 / 7200

I = 0.67 A

Practice Questions

1. Calculate the mass of lead deposited when a current of 3 A flows through molten PbBr₂ for 1 hour.

Show Answer
Half-equation: Pb²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Pb (2 mol e⁻ : 1 mol Pb)
Q = I × t = 3 × 3600 = 10,800 C
Moles e⁻ = 10800/96500 = 0.112 mol
Moles Pb = 0.112/2 = 0.056 mol
Mass Pb = 0.056 × 207 = 11.6 g

2. What volume of hydrogen gas (at RTP) is produced at the cathode when a current of 5 A passes through dilute H₂SO₄ for 20 minutes?

Show Answer
Half-equation: 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → H₂ (2 mol e⁻ : 1 mol H₂)
Q = I × t = 5 × (20 × 60) = 6000 C
Moles e⁻ = 6000/96500 = 0.0622 mol
Moles H₂ = 0.0622/2 = 0.0311 mol
Volume H₂ = 0.0311 × 24 = 0.746 dm³ = 746 cm³

3. How long must a current of 2 A flow to deposit 3.18 g of copper from CuSO₄ solution?

Show Answer
Half-equation: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu (2 mol e⁻ : 1 mol Cu)
Moles Cu = 3.18/63.5 = 0.05 mol
Moles e⁻ = 0.05 × 2 = 0.1 mol
Q = 0.1 × 96500 = 9650 C
t = Q/I = 9650/2 = 4825 s = 80.4 min = 1 hour 20 min

4. A current of 1.5 A flows through AgNO₃ solution. Calculate the mass of silver deposited in 45 minutes.

Show Answer
Half-equation: Ag⁺ + e⁻ → Ag (1 mol e⁻ : 1 mol Ag)
Q = I × t = 1.5 × (45 × 60) = 4050 C
Moles e⁻ = 4050/96500 = 0.042 mol
Moles Ag = 0.042 mol (same ratio)
Mass Ag = 0.042 × 108 = 4.54 g

Key Takeaways

  • • Q = I × t (charge = current × time in seconds)
  • • Moles of electrons = Q / 96,500 (Faraday's constant)
  • • Always write the half-equation to find the mole ratio
  • • Mass = moles × Mr; Gas volume = moles × 24 dm³
  • • The number of electrons (n) equals the ion charge
