Galvanic (Voltaic) Cells

Understanding electrochemical cells that convert chemical energy into electrical energy spontaneously.

Learning Objectives

  • • Explain how a galvanic cell produces electricity
  • • Identify the anode and cathode in a galvanic cell
  • • Write cell notation using standard conventions
  • • Calculate cell potential from standard electrode potentials

1 What is a Galvanic Cell?

A Galvanic (Voltaic) cell is an electrochemical cell that converts chemical energy from spontaneous redox reactions into electrical energy. Unlike electrolysis, galvanic cells generate electricity naturally!

Key Characteristics:

  • • Uses a spontaneous redox reaction
  • • Converts chemical energy → electrical energy
  • • Produces a positive cell potential (E°cell > 0)
  • • Electrons flow from anode to cathode through external circuit

Galvanic Cell

  • • Spontaneous reaction
  • • Produces electricity
  • • No external power needed
  • • E°cell is positive
  • • Chemical → Electrical energy
  • Examples: Batteries, fuel cells

Electrolytic Cell

  • • Non-spontaneous reaction
  • • Uses electricity
  • • External power required
  • • Applied voltage overcomes E°cell
  • • Electrical → Chemical energy
  • Examples: Electroplating, electrolysis

2 Parts of a Galvanic Cell

4(aq) CuSO4(aq) Zn Cu V Salt Bridge ANODE (-) CATHODE (+) e⁻ flow → Zn²⁺ Cu²⁺

Daniel Cell: A common type of galvanic cell

Anode (Negative Electrode)

  • • Where oxidation occurs
  • • Electrons flow OUT from anode
  • • Metal electrode dissolves (loses mass)
  • • In Daniel Cell: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻

Cathode (Positive Electrode)

  • • Where reduction occurs
  • • Electrons flow INTO cathode
  • • Metal deposits on electrode (gains mass)
  • • In Daniel Cell: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu

Salt Bridge

  • • Contains inert electrolyte (e.g., KNO₃, KCl)
  • • Completes the circuit by allowing ion flow
  • • Maintains electrical neutrality in each half-cell
  • • Anions flow to anode; Cations flow to cathode

3 Cell Notation (Shorthand)

Cell notation is a shorthand way to represent a galvanic cell without drawing a diagram.

General Format:

Anode | Anode ion || Cathode ion | Cathode
  • • | = Phase boundary (between solid and solution)
  • • || = Salt bridge (separates two half-cells)
  • • Anode (oxidation) written on the left
  • • Cathode (reduction) written on the right
Zn(s) | Zn²⁺(aq) || Cu²⁺(aq) | Cu(s)

Zn(s) | Zn²⁺(aq) → Anode half-cell (oxidation: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻)

|| → Salt bridge

Cu²⁺(aq) | Cu(s) → Cathode half-cell (reduction: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu)

4 Calculating Cell Potential

The cell potential (voltage) tells us the "driving force" of the reaction. It's calculated using standard electrode potentials (E° values).

Formula:

E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode

Or: E°cell = E°reduction - E°oxidation

Important Points

  • • For a spontaneous cell, E°cell must be positive
  • • Standard electrode potentials are measured at 25°C, 1 M, 1 atm
  • • More positive E° = stronger oxidizing agent (better at gaining e⁻)
  • • More negative E° = stronger reducing agent (better at losing e⁻)

Given: E°(Zn²⁺/Zn) = -0.76 V and E°(Cu²⁺/Cu) = +0.34 V

Step 1: Identify cathode and anode

Cu has more positive E°, so Cu²⁺ is reduced (cathode)
Zn is oxidized (anode)

Step 2: Apply the formula

E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode

E°cell = (+0.34 V) - (-0.76 V)

E°cell = +1.10 V

Answer: E°cell = +1.10 V (spontaneous)

Practice Problems

1. In a galvanic cell, electrons flow from the _____ to the _____ through the external circuit.

Show Answer
Anode to Cathode - The anode is where oxidation occurs (loses electrons), and electrons flow to the cathode where reduction occurs (gains electrons).

2. Write the cell notation for a cell with Mg(s)|Mg²⁺(aq) as the anode and Ag⁺(aq)|Ag(s) as the cathode.

Show Answer
Mg(s) | Mg²⁺(aq) || Ag⁺(aq) | Ag(s)

3. Calculate E°cell if E°(Mg²⁺/Mg) = -2.37 V and E°(Ag⁺/Ag) = +0.80 V.

Show Answer

E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode

E°cell = (+0.80 V) - (-2.37 V)

E°cell = +3.17 V

Key Takeaways

  • • Galvanic cells convert chemical energy to electrical energy spontaneously
  • • Anode = oxidation (negative); Cathode = reduction (positive)
  • • Electrons flow: Anode → external circuit → Cathode
  • • Cell notation: Anode | Anode ion || Cathode ion | Cathode
  • • E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode (must be positive for spontaneous reaction)
