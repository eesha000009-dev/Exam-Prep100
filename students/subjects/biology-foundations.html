Foundations — Classification & Cell Biology

Why Study This First?

Cells and classification are the language and units of biology. Without a firm cell-level understanding, physiology and ecology won't make sense.

Prerequisites

Basic chemistry (water, macromolecules) — if absent, teach a short recap.

Topics

  • Classification / Organisation of Life (kingdoms, basic taxonomy concepts)
  • Cell 1: Living Unit — Structure (ultrastructure: nucleus, organelles, membranes)
  • Cell 2: Properties & Functions (cell membrane, transport, organelle functions)
  • Supporting systems and mechanisms (basic tissues → organs → organ systems)

Key Practicals

  • Microscope work (prepare and view onion/cheek cells)
  • Staining (iodine, methylene blue)
  • Drawing labeled diagrams
  • Cell counting estimates
