Ecology & Ecosystems

Why Study This?

Moves from organism to environment — necessary for understanding biodiversity, resources, and human impact.

Topics

  • Basic ecological concepts (population, community, trophic levels)
  • Functioning ecosystem (energy flow, food chains/webs, nutrient cycles)
  • Ecology of population (growth curves, carrying capacity)
  • Aquatic and terrestrial habitats (adaptations, zonation)
  • Ecological management (conservation, sustainable practices)

Key Practicals / Fieldwork

  • Transect/quadrat sampling
  • Population estimates
  • Food web construction
  • Water/soil sampling and basic analysis
  • Habitat surveys
