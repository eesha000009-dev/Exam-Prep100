1 What is a Cell?

A cell is the smallest structural and functional unit of life. All living organisms are made up of cells. Some organisms (like bacteria) are unicellular (single cell), while others (like humans) are multicellular (many cells).

The Cell Theory

  1. All living things are composed of cells
  2. The cell is the basic unit of life
  3. All cells arise from pre-existing cells

Plant Cells

  • • Have a cell wall (cellulose)
  • • Contain chloroplasts
  • • Large permanent vacuole
  • • Regular, fixed shape

Animal Cells

  • • No cell wall
  • • No chloroplasts
  • • Small temporary vacuoles
  • • Irregular shape

2 Cell Organelles and Their Functions

Each organelle has a specific function that helps the cell survive and perform its tasks. Here are the key organelles you need to know:

Nucleus

The "control center" of the cell. Contains DNA (genetic material) and controls cell activities like growth, metabolism, and reproduction. Surrounded by a nuclear membrane with pores.

Mitochondria

The "powerhouse" of the cell. Site of cellular respiration where glucose is broken down to release energy in the form of ATP. Has a double membrane with inner folds called cristae.

Ribosomes

Tiny structures that are the site of protein synthesis. Found free in cytoplasm or attached to endoplasmic reticulum. Made of RNA and protein.

Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)

Rough ER has ribosomes attached and transports proteins. Smooth ER has no ribosomes and makes lipids. Forms a network of membranes throughout the cell.

Golgi Apparatus

The "post office" of the cell. Modifies, packages, and secretes proteins and lipids. Made of stacked, flattened membrane sacs called cisternae.

Cell Membrane

A selectively permeable membrane that controls what enters and leaves the cell. Made of a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins. Protects the cell and maintains homeostasis.

Chloroplasts (Plants Only)

Site of photosynthesis where sunlight is converted into food (glucose). Contains green pigment called chlorophyll. Has a double membrane with internal thylakoid membranes.

Cell Wall (Plants Only)

A rigid outer layer made of cellulose that provides structural support and protection. Fully permeable to most substances. Gives plant cells their fixed shape.

Vacuole

Storage sac for water, nutrients, and waste. Plant cells have a large central vacuole that helps maintain turgor pressure. Animal cells have smaller, temporary vacuoles.

Practice Questions

1. Which organelle is responsible for protein synthesis?

Show Answer
Ribosomes - They are the site where amino acids are assembled into proteins based on instructions from mRNA.

2. State three differences between plant and animal cells.

Show Answer
  1. Plant cells have a cell wall; animal cells do not
  2. Plant cells have chloroplasts; animal cells do not
  3. Plant cells have a large permanent vacuole; animal cells have small temporary vacuoles

3. Why is the mitochondria called the "powerhouse" of the cell?

Show Answer
Mitochondria are called the "powerhouse" because they produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate) through cellular respiration. ATP is the energy currency of the cell, used for all cellular activities.

4. What is the function of the cell membrane?

Show Answer
The cell membrane:
  • Controls what enters and leaves the cell (selectively permeable)
  • Protects the cell's internal environment
  • Maintains homeostasis
  • Contains receptors for cell signaling

Key Takeaways

  • • Cells are the basic unit of life - all living things are made of cells
  • • Plant and animal cells share many organelles but have key differences
  • • Each organelle has a specific function essential for cell survival
  • • The nucleus controls cell activities; mitochondria produce energy
  • • Plant cells uniquely have cell walls, chloroplasts, and large vacuoles
