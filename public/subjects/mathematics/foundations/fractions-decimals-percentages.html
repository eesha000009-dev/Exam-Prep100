Fractions, Decimals & Percentages

Master the essential skills of converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages - fundamental concepts for WAEC examination success.

Learning Objectives

  • • Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • • Perform calculations with fractions (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division)
  • • Calculate percentage increases and decreases
  • • Apply percentage to real-world problems (profit, loss, discount)

1 Understanding Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of a numerator (top number) and denominator (bottom number).

Numerator / Denominator

Example: 3/4 means "3 parts out of 4 equal parts"

Proper Fraction

Numerator < Denominator

e.g., 3/4, 2/5

Improper Fraction

Numerator > Denominator

e.g., 7/4, 11/5

Mixed Number

Whole number + Fraction

e.g., 1¾, 2⅗

Problem: Convert 17/5 to a mixed number.

Step 1: Divide numerator by denominator

17 ÷ 5 = 3 remainder 2

Step 2: Write as mixed number

Whole number = 3
Remainder = 2
Therefore: 17/5 = 3⅖

Answer: 17/5 = 3⅖

2 Fraction Operations

Addition & Subtraction

Find a common denominator first!

a/b + c/d = (ad + bc) / bd

Multiplication

Multiply numerators and denominators

a/b × c/d = (a×c) / (b×d)

Problem: Calculate 2/3 + 5/6

Step 1: Find common denominator (LCM of 3 and 6 = 6)

Step 2: Convert fractions

2/3 = 4/6 (multiply top and bottom by 2)
5/6 = 5/6 (already has denominator 6)

Step 3: Add numerators

4/6 + 5/6 = (4 + 5)/6 = 9/6 = 3/2 = 1½

Answer: 2/3 + 5/6 = 1½

Problem: Calculate 3/4 ÷ 2/5

Rule: To divide, multiply by the reciprocal (flip the second fraction)

3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 × 5/2
= (3 × 5) / (4 × 2)
= 15/8
= 1⅞

Answer: 3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 1⅞

3 Decimals and Conversions

Decimals are another way to represent parts of a whole. They are based on powers of 10.

Converting Fractions to Decimals:

Divide the numerator by the denominator

1/2 = 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5

3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75

1/3 = 1 ÷ 3 = 0.333... (repeating)

Problem: Convert 0.375 to a fraction in its simplest form.

Step 1: Write as fraction over power of 10

0.375 = 375/1000

Step 2: Simplify by dividing by common factors

375/1000 = 75/200 (÷5)
= 15/40 (÷5)
= 3/8 (÷5)

Answer: 0.375 = 3/8

4 Percentages

Percent means "per hundred". The symbol % means "out of 100".

Key Conversions:

Fraction → Decimal → Percentage
1/4 = 0.25 = 25%
1/2 = 0.5 = 50%
3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
1/5 = 0.2 = 20%

Percentage Change Formula

% Change = (New Value - Original Value) / Original Value × 100

Problem: A shirt's price increased from ₦2,000 to ₦2,500. Find the percentage increase.

Step 1: Find the increase

Increase = 2,500 - 2,000 = ₦500

Step 2: Calculate percentage

% Increase = (500 / 2,000) × 100
= 0.25 × 100
= 25%

Answer: 25% increase

Profit

Selling Price > Cost Price

Profit = SP - CP

% Profit = Profit/CP × 100

Loss

Selling Price < Cost Price

Loss = CP - SP

% Loss = Loss/CP × 100

Example: A trader buys goods for ₦400 and sells for ₦500.

Profit = 500 - 400 = ₦100
% Profit = (100/400) × 100 = 25%
