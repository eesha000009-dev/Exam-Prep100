Sentence Structure

Understanding subject, predicate, objects and complements; simple and complex sentences.

Worked example

Identify the subject and predicate: "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

Solution: Subject: "The quick brown fox". Predicate: "jumps over the lazy dog".

Practice

  1. Label subject and predicate: "Rain fell all night."
  2. Transform to a complex sentence: "She left early." add a reason clause.

Brief answers

  1. Subject: "Rain"; Predicate: "fell all night."
  2. Example: "She left early because she was unwell."
