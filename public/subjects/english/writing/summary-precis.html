Summary & Précis Writing

Key Principles

  • Identifying main ideas
  • Distinguishing essential from non-essential details
  • Maintaining objectivity
  • Using own words
  • Following word limits
  • Précis vs Summary:
    • Précis: formal, structured, exact proportion
    • Summary: flexible, focused on key points

Worked Example

Original Passage (150 words):

The advent of artificial intelligence has sparked both excitement and concern across various sectors of society. In healthcare, AI algorithms are revolutionizing diagnosis and treatment planning, enabling doctors to detect diseases earlier and develop more personalized treatment approaches. Educational institutions are exploring AI-powered adaptive learning systems that can adjust to individual student needs and learning styles. However, these advancements also raise significant ethical questions about privacy, decision-making autonomy, and the potential displacement of human workers. Critics argue that over-reliance on AI could lead to a decrease in human expertise and judgment. Furthermore, there are concerns about bias in AI systems and their impact on marginalized communities. As we continue to integrate AI into critical systems, it becomes increasingly important to establish robust regulatory frameworks and ethical guidelines to ensure responsible development and deployment.

Summary (50 words):

AI technology brings both benefits and challenges across sectors. While it enhances healthcare diagnosis and personalized education, concerns exist about privacy, autonomy, and workforce displacement. Critics worry about diminishing human expertise and algorithmic bias. This necessitates strong regulatory and ethical frameworks for responsible AI development.

Practice Questions

1. Summarize this passage in 40-50 words:

Climate change mitigation requires immediate global action. Rising temperatures are causing sea levels to rise, threatening coastal communities and ecosystems. Extreme weather events are becoming more frequent and intense. While renewable energy adoption is increasing, current efforts fall short of international climate goals. Many countries continue to rely heavily on fossil fuels despite their environmental impact. Scientists warn that without dramatic reductions in greenhouse gas emissions, we risk catastrophic environmental changes.

2. Write a précis of this passage, maintaining the key points and tone:

Digital literacy has become essential in the modern workplace. Employees must navigate various software platforms, communicate through digital channels, and adapt to rapidly changing technologies. Despite this necessity, many workers, particularly in older age groups, struggle with digital tools. Organizations must invest in comprehensive digital training programs to ensure their workforce remains competitive and productive.

Brief Solutions

1. Climate change poses urgent global threats through rising sea levels and extreme weather events. Despite increasing renewable energy adoption, continued fossil fuel dependence hampers progress toward climate goals. Scientists emphasize the need for dramatic emissions reduction to prevent catastrophic changes.

2. The author argues that while digital literacy is crucial for modern workplace success, many workers struggle with technology adoption. This necessitates organizational investment in digital training to maintain workforce competitiveness.
